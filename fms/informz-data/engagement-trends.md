**Chart window:** Jan 2024 → May 2026 (~28 months)
**Series:** A (blue) / B (orange) / C (red) / D (green) / F (gray)

The five engagement tiers plotted as subscriber count over time.

## Key finding for the board

**F has been the dominant tier for the entire 2+ year window.** This isn't a recent slide. The list has been mostly disengaged since at least January 2024. Whatever campaigns FMS has been running over those 2+ years have not moved the engagement-tier distribution materially.

What did move:
- **C-tier shrank from ~3,500 (Jan 2024) to ~2,100 (May 2026)** — a ~40% loss of moderately engaged contacts. Most of them migrated DOWN to D and F, not up to A/B.
- **A-tier briefly spiked to ~600-700 in Mar/Apr 2026** then dropped back to ~150-200. Worth investigating internally: was there a Forum-related campaign that briefly moved people up?

## What this means

1. **The re-engagement campaign drafted in `docs/fms/campaigns/reengagement.md` should target both C and D tiers, not just C-and-disengaged as currently scoped.** D-tier (731 contacts) is the more recoverable subset of disengaged; F (5,694) is the harder sell.
2. **The brief Mar/Apr 2026 A-tier spike + collapse suggests engagement IS movable** with the right campaign. The lift was real; it just didn't stick. The next campaign should be paired with a retention mechanism (recurring touchpoint, peer cohort, etc.) to hold gains.
3. **The status quo has been remarkably stable.** Roughly the same A/B/C/D/F percentage shape for 28 months means current activity isn't differentiating engagement. The board should hear this as "the current motion is treading water, by data."
