AEO vs SEO comes down to what you are optimizing for: SEO (search engine optimization) works to rank your website in the list of links on Google, while AEO (answer engine optimization) works to get your business named inside an AI generated answer from tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. The two overlap heavily and reinforce each other. For contractors in 2026, you need both.

## TL;DR: Key Takeaways

- **SEO** aims to rank your pages in a list of links. **AEO** aims to get your business named in an AI answer.
- They overlap heavily, and good SEO supports AEO, but AEO puts more weight on clarity, structured data, and trust than on keywords and backlinks alone.
- A search results page has ten spots. An AI answer usually names three, so the margin for being unclear is smaller.
- About 60 percent of home service searches now start with an AI tool ([BrightLocal, 2025](https://www.brightlocal.com)), which is why AEO can no longer be ignored.
- The good news: the same foundation work serves both at once.

## What is SEO for contractors?

SEO is the practice of getting your website to rank higher in traditional search results. For a contractor, that means appearing on the first page of Google when a homeowner searches "plumber near me" or "AC repair [city]." The main levers are keywords, content, technical health, page speed, and backlinks from other reputable sites.

SEO has been the backbone of online lead generation for two decades, and it still matters. A homeowner who clicks a top organic result is a real, free lead. But it is no longer the whole game, because many homeowners now get their answer before they ever see a list of links.

## What is AEO for contractors?

AEO is the practice of getting your business named and recommended by AI answer engines. When a homeowner asks ChatGPT or Gemini "who is the best electrician near me," the assistant returns a short answer naming a few companies. AEO is the work of becoming one of those names.

It overlaps with SEO but rewards different things. AEO leans heavily on clarity (plainly stating what you do and where), structured data (schema the machine can read), a complete Google Business Profile, and a steady flow of reviews. The aim is not to rank a page but to be the business the AI is confident enough to name.

## AEO vs SEO: the key differences

Here is how the two compare for a home service business.

| Factor | SEO | AEO |
|---|---|---|
| The goal | Rank your page in a list | Get named in the answer |
| What the homeowner sees | Ten links to choose from | An answer naming 2 to 3 businesses |
| Biggest levers | Keywords, content, backlinks, speed | Clarity, schema, profile, reviews |
| Number of winners | A page of ten | Usually three |
| Risk of being unclear | You rank lower | You are left out entirely |

The takeaway: SEO gives ten businesses a shot on page one. An AI answer names three. That smaller window is why the fundamentals matter more under AEO, not less.

## Is AEO the same as GEO?

You will also see the term GEO, or generative engine optimization. In practice the two describe nearly the same work. AEO is usually used for optimizing toward direct answers and featured snippets, while GEO is used for the broader goal of being cited across generative AI tools. For a contractor the playbook is identical, so do not get hung up on the label.

## Do contractors need both AEO and SEO?

Yes, and the reason is that they reinforce each other. Strong SEO, a fast, well-structured site with good content, also makes you easier for AI assistants to read and trust. A complete Google Business Profile helps you rank locally and helps you get named in AI answers. The work is not two separate budgets so much as one foundation that pays off in two places.

Splitting them into "do SEO or do AEO" is a false choice. The contractors winning now treat them as a single system: get the foundation right, and you show up both in the list of links and in the AI answer above it.

## Which should a contractor focus on first?

Start with the foundation that serves both. A complete, accurate Google Business Profile, a clear website that states your trade and service area, schema markup, and a steady review habit improve your SEO and your AEO at the same time. There is no version of this where that foundation is wasted.

From there, lean into whichever gap is costing you more. If you have no organic presence, invest in SEO content. If homeowners in your area are asking AI and getting competitors' names, prioritize the AEO signals. ## What mistakes do contractors make with AEO and SEO?

- **Treating them as either/or.** They are one foundation, not competing budgets.
- **Chasing keywords while ignoring the profile.** Your Google Business Profile drives both local SEO and AEO, and it is often half-empty.
- **Skipping schema.** Most contractor sites have none, which quietly caps both their rich-result eligibility and their AEO clarity.
- **Letting reviews go stale.** Recent reviews feed local ranking and AI trust at once.

## What does AEO vs SEO look like in practice?

Picture the same homeowner two ways. In the first, they search Google for "electrician near me" and scan a page of links and a map, then click two or three to compare. That is the SEO world, and your job is to rank high enough on that page to earn the click. In the second, they ask ChatGPT "who is a good electrician in my area," and the assistant names a couple of companies in a sentence or two. That is the AEO world, and your job is to be one of the names.

Notice that the same business can win one and lose the other. A contractor with a fast, keyword-rich website might rank well on Google yet never get named by an assistant, because the assistant could not find a complete profile or clear, machine-readable facts about the business. Another contractor with a thinner site but a complete Google Business Profile and strong reviews might get named by the AI while ranking poorly in traditional search. The goal is to win both, and the way you do that is by building the foundation each one rewards.

## Why the overlap between AEO and SEO works in your favor

The encouraging part of AEO vs SEO is how much the two share. A fast, well-structured website helps you rank and helps an assistant read you. A complete Google Business Profile drives local ranking and feeds AI recommendations. Recent reviews lift your local position and build the trust an assistant needs to name you. Schema markup earns rich results in search and gives machines the clarity AEO depends on. Almost every foundational move pays off twice.

That overlap means you are not choosing between two budgets so much as building one foundation that shows up in two places. The contractors who treat AEO and SEO as a single system, rather than competing line items, get more out of every hour and dollar they invest, because the work compounds across both the list of links and the answer above it.

## A simple way to think about AEO vs SEO

Here is a plain analogy. SEO is like making sure your business is listed in the right section of a very long directory, positioned so a homeowner flipping through it finds you. AEO is like being the name a knowledgeable clerk gives when a customer walks up and asks, "who should I call for this." The directory still matters, but more and more homeowners are walking straight to the clerk.

You want both. You want to be listed where people look, and you want to be the name the assistant recommends. Build the shared foundation, and you earn a place in the directory and a place in the answer, which is exactly where home service buying decisions are now being made.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the difference between AEO and SEO?

SEO works to rank your website in a list of search results. AEO works to get your business named inside an AI generated answer. SEO optimizes for position on a page of links; AEO optimizes for being one of the few businesses an assistant recommends. They overlap, and good SEO supports AEO.

### Is SEO still worth it for contractors in 2026?

Yes. Many homeowners still use traditional search, and organic clicks are real, free leads. SEO also strengthens the same site signals that AEO depends on. It is not obsolete, it is one half of a larger system that now includes AI answers.

### Does AEO replace SEO?

No. AEO is a layer on top of strong fundamentals, not a replacement. The website, content, and local signals that power SEO also feed the assistants. Doing AEO well almost always means doing the SEO basics well too.

### Can I do AEO and SEO at the same time?

Yes, and you should. The core work, a complete Google Business Profile, clear website copy, schema, and steady reviews, improves both simultaneously. There is very little you can do for AEO that does not also help your SEO.

### How long do AEO and SEO take to work?

Both compound. Profile and schema changes can register within weeks, while content, reviews, and authority build over one to three months. Neither is instant, and both pay back over time.

### Which matters more for a home service business?

Neither universally. SEO captures homeowners using traditional search; AEO captures the growing share using AI. The right answer is to build the shared foundation so you show up in both, then weight your effort toward whichever gap is costing you jobs.
