AEO for contractors is the practice of optimizing a home service business so AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews name and recommend it when a homeowner asks for help. Where SEO aims to rank your website in a list of links, AEO aims to get your company named inside the answer itself.

## TL;DR: Key Takeaways

- **AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization:** getting your business named in AI generated answers, not just ranked in a list of links.
- Roughly 60 percent of home service searches now start with an AI tool ([BrightLocal, 2025](https://www.brightlocal.com)), and AI answers usually name only two or three companies per trade and city.
- AEO rewards clarity, consistency, structured data, and reviews more than keywords and backlinks alone.
- It is not pay to play. AI assistants cannot be paid to recommend you.
- The fastest place for a contractor to start is a complete Google Business Profile plus schema markup on the website.

## What does AEO stand for?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization. An answer engine is any tool that gives a direct answer instead of a page of links: ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google's AI Overviews all qualify. AEO is the work of making your business the one those tools name when someone asks a question you can solve.

For a contractor, that question is usually some version of "who is the best plumber, electrician, HVAC, or roofing company near me." The answer engine replies with a short list, often just two or three businesses. AEO is how you become one of them.

## How is AEO different from SEO?

SEO and AEO overlap, and good SEO still helps. But they optimize for different end results. Here is the comparison for a home service business.

| Factor | SEO (search engine optimization) | AEO (answer engine optimization) |
|---|---|---|
| The goal | Rank your page in a list of links | Get your business named in the answer |
| What the homeowner sees | Ten blue links to choose from | A short answer naming 2 to 3 companies |
| Biggest levers | Keywords, backlinks, page speed | Clarity, consistency, structured data, reviews |
| How many winners | Page one fits about ten | The answer usually names three |
| The risk of being unclear | You rank lower | You are left out entirely |

The short version: SEO gives ten companies a shot on page one. An AI answer typically names three. That smaller window is exactly why the fundamentals (clear copy, consistent listings, structured data, strong reviews) matter more under AEO, not less.

## How is AEO different from GEO?

You may also see the term GEO, or Generative Engine Optimization. In practice the two terms describe nearly the same work. AEO is usually used to mean optimizing for direct answers and featured snippets, while GEO is used for the broader goal of being cited and recommended across generative AI tools. For a contractor, the practical playbook is identical, so do not get hung up on the label. The point is the same: be the business the AI names.

## Why does AEO matter for home service businesses now?

Because the way homeowners find contractors has shifted, and most businesses have not caught up. According to [BrightLocal's 2025 research](https://www.brightlocal.com), roughly 60 percent of home service searches now begin with an AI tool rather than a traditional search engine, and that share grows every quarter.

When a homeowner asks ChatGPT for the best contractor in their city, the assistant names a few companies and the homeowner calls one. If your business is not in that answer, you are not losing the comparison. You are not in it. The job goes to whoever the AI mentioned, and you never knew the opportunity existed.

This is why AEO is urgent rather than optional. The businesses that get named win jobs they never had to bid on. The ones that are absent lose jobs invisibly, with no traffic report to warn them.

## How do AI answer engines choose which contractors to name?

Answer engines build their replies from the information they can find and trust about businesses in an area. They lean on a few sources in particular:

- **Your Google Business Profile**, because it is structured and trusted.
- **Your website**, especially clear statements of what you do, where, and for whom.
- **Your reviews**, weighing volume, recency, and how you respond.
- **Structured data (schema)** on your site that labels your services and service area for machines.
- **Mentions across the web**, from local news to industry directories, that confirm you are real and consistent.

The pattern is simple. Answer engines reward the business that is easiest to understand and verify. Clarity and consistency beat budget.

## How can a contractor start with AEO?

You do not need a large budget to begin. You need to set the foundation an AI looks for. Start with these five moves, roughly in order of speed-to-impact.

1. **Complete your Google Business Profile.** Claim it, fill every field, list all services, set your service area and hours, and add real photos. This is the single highest-impact afternoon of work.
2. **Make your website answer the obvious questions.** State your trade, your cities, the brands you service, and whether you take emergency calls, in plain language an AI can quote.
3. **Add schema markup.** Use structured data to declare your business type, services, and service area. Most contractor sites have none, which leaves the AI guessing.
4. **Build a steady flow of recent reviews.** Ask after every job, respond to each one, and keep it consistent. Recency signals that you are active.
5. **Keep your name, address, and phone number identical everywhere.** Mismatches across your site, Google, and directories make the AI less confident in naming you.

## What kinds of contractors benefit most from AEO?

Any local service business that homeowners search for by trade and city benefits, but the impact is largest where the job value is high and the decision is urgent. Plumbing, HVAC, electrical, and roofing all fit: a homeowner with a burst pipe, a dead furnace, a tripped panel, or a leaking roof wants help now and trusts the first credible name they get.

Established companies see the most return, because they already have the reviews, the history, and the service quality to back up the recommendation. AEO does not invent a reputation. It makes the reputation you have earned visible to the tools homeowners now use.

## What are the most common AEO mistakes contractors make?

Most contractors are not getting AEO wrong on purpose. They simply never set the foundation an answer engine needs. These are the patterns that quietly keep good companies out of AI answers.

- **A brochure website.** The site talks about being "trusted local experts" but never states the service area, the brands serviced, or whether emergency calls are answered. The AI has nothing specific to quote.
- **A thin or unclaimed Google Business Profile.** No hours, no full service list, no recent photos. This is the most common reason an established company is missing from AI answers.
- **No structured data.** Without schema, a machine has to infer your business from marketing copy, and inference is where you lose to the company that spelled it out.
- **Stale reviews.** A burst of reviews from two years ago followed by silence reads as a business that has gone quiet.
- **Inconsistent listings.** A phone number on the website that does not match Yelp or an old directory chips away at the AI's confidence in naming you.
- **Chasing every acronym instead of the fundamentals.** Owners read about AEO, GEO, and SEO and freeze. The fundamentals are the same. Clarity, consistency, structured data, and reviews carry almost all of the weight.

Fix these and you are ahead of most of your market, because most of your competitors have not.

## Do you need an agency to do AEO?

No. The foundational work is mostly time and attention, and a motivated owner can complete a Google Business Profile, tighten website copy, and start a review habit without help. Where an agency earns its keep is speed and compounding: setting up schema correctly, keeping listings consistent at scale, publishing content that answers real questions, and maintaining all of it while you run your crews. If you have the time, start yourself. If you would rather it be done right and kept current, that is the case for help.

## How much does AEO cost for a contractor?

There is no placement fee, because you cannot buy your way into an AI answer. The cost is in the work. Done yourself, the foundational moves cost mostly time: an afternoon on your Google Business Profile, a few hours tightening website copy, and an ongoing habit of asking for reviews. Done with help, expect costs to track with scope. A one-time audit and strategy typically runs in the low four figures, while ongoing schema, content, listing management, and reputation work are usually handled on a monthly retainer.

The honest way to think about it is return, not price. A single furnace replacement, panel upgrade, or roof job can be worth thousands of dollars, and the lifetime value of the customer behind it is worth far more. If clarity and consistency get your name into even a handful of AI answers a month, the foundational work pays for itself quickly. Spend where it compounds: the profile, the structured data, the reviews, and the content that answers real homeowner questions.

## Frequently asked questions

### What does AEO stand for?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization. It is the practice of optimizing your business so AI tools that give direct answers, such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews, name and recommend you.

### Is AEO the same as SEO?

No, though they overlap. SEO works to rank your web pages in a list of links. AEO works to get your business named inside an AI generated answer. Good SEO supports AEO, but an AI answer usually names only two or three companies, so clarity and trust signals carry more weight.

### Can I pay to be recommended by ChatGPT or other AI tools?

No. AI assistants cannot be paid for placement, and ad spend does not buy a spot in their answers. Recommendation is earned through clarity, consistency, reviews, and trust. Any service promising to buy your way into AI answers is selling something that does not exist.

### How long does AEO take to work?

It depends on your starting point. Profile and schema fixes can register within a few weeks. Reviews and external mentions compound over one to three months. Foundation work pays back fastest, and authority work builds over time.

### Do I need to optimize for ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini separately?

Mostly no. The same foundations, a complete Google Business Profile, clear website copy, schema markup, and strong reviews, help across all major AI assistants at once. You do not need a separate strategy for each tool.

### Does AEO replace my regular SEO and ads?

No. AEO works alongside them. Your website, reviews, and even your ad-driven traffic all feed the signals that answer engines read. AEO is the layer that makes sure all of that work also shows up where homeowners are now searching.

### How do I know if AEO is working for my business?

Ask the assistants directly. Type "best [your trade] in [your city]" into ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini and see whether your name appears. Tracking that over time, alongside your booked jobs and call volume, is the clearest signal.
