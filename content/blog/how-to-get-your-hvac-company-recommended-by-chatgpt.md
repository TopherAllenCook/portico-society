## Why homeowners now ask AI for HVAC recommendations

A homeowner with a dead furnace used to open Google, or grab the phone book before that. Today a growing share of them open ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini and type "who is the best HVAC company near me." They read the two or three names the AI gives back, and they call the first one.

If your company is not one of those names, you were never in the running. The homeowner does not compare you to the competition and pick someone else. They simply never learn you exist. The job goes to whoever the AI decided to mention.

This shift is happening fast. The companies named in those answers win jobs they never had to bid on. The companies that are absent lose jobs they never knew existed.

## How do AI assistants decide which HVAC companies to recommend?

When someone asks for the best HVAC company in a city, the AI is not reading from a secret ranking. It synthesizes an answer from the information it can find and trust about businesses in that area: your website, your [Google Business Profile](https://support.google.com/business), your reviews, directory listings, and anywhere else your name appears online.

The businesses that get named consistently share a few traits:

- Their business name, address, and phone number match everywhere they appear.
- Their website states plainly what they do, where they do it, and who they serve.
- They have a healthy base of recent reviews.
- Their site uses structured data that spells out their services and service area in a format machines can read without guessing.

In short, AI assistants reward clarity and consistency. The company that makes it easy to understand exactly what it offers and where gets recommended. The company that hides that behind vague copy and a brochure website gets skipped.

## How do you get your HVAC company recommended by ChatGPT?

There are seven moves that meaningfully improve the odds an AI names your company. None require a big budget. They require attention to the foundation.

1. **Claim and complete your Google Business Profile.** Make it accurate and full: services, hours, service area, and real photos of your team and work. AI leans on this profile because it is structured and trusted.
2. **Make your website answer the obvious questions.** What services do you offer? What is your service area? Do you take emergency calls? Which brands do you install and repair? When the answers are clear, the AI can quote them directly.
3. **Add schema markup (structured data).** This is code that labels your content so machines understand it. Schema can declare that you are a heating and cooling contractor, that you serve specific cities, and that you offer furnace repair and AC installation. Most HVAC sites have none, which leaves the AI guessing.
4. **Build a steady flow of recent reviews.** Ask every satisfied customer, respond to the ones you get, and keep it consistent. A company with 200 reviews from the last year reads very differently than one with 12 from three years ago.
5. **Keep your NAP consistent everywhere.** Name, address, and phone number should be identical across your site, Google, Yelp, and every directory. Mismatches make the AI less confident in naming you.
6. **Earn mentions on sources the AI already trusts.** Local news, industry directories, supplier partner pages, and community sites all add weight to your entity.
7. **Publish content that answers real homeowner questions.** Helpful pages about common repairs, costs, and decisions give the AI more of your words to pull from and cite.

### What "clear" actually looks like

The difference between getting named and getting skipped often comes down to one sentence on your website. Compare these two.

- **Vague:** "We are your trusted local comfort experts, dedicated to quality service and customer satisfaction."
- **Clear:** "We are a family-owned HVAC contractor serving Austin and Round Rock. We install and repair Carrier, Trane, and Lennox systems, and we answer emergency calls 24 hours a day."

The first sentence could describe any company in any city. The second gives an AI specific, quotable facts: the trade, the cities, the brands, and the emergency availability. When an assistant builds an answer, it reaches for the business it can describe precisely. Write for that.

## ChatGPT vs Google: how getting found is different

Traditional SEO and answer engine optimization (AEO) overlap, but they reward different things. Here is how they compare for a home service business.

| Factor | Google Search (SEO) | ChatGPT and AI search (AEO) |
|---|---|---|
| What the user sees | A list of links to choose from | A short answer naming 2 to 3 businesses |
| How you win | Rank on page one | Get named in the answer |
| Biggest levers | Keywords, backlinks, page speed | Clarity, consistency, structured data, reviews |
| Room at the top | 10 blue links | Usually 3 names per city and trade |
| Who notices you lost | You see traffic drop | You never know the job existed |

The takeaway: AEO is winner-take-few. Where Google gives ten companies a shot on page one, an AI answer typically names three. That is why getting your foundation right matters more, not less.

## What keeps most HVAC companies out of AI answers

Most owners are not losing AI visibility because they did something wrong. They are losing it because they never set the foundation an AI needs to trust them. These are the patterns we see most often.

- **A brochure website.** The site describes the company but never states the service area, the brands serviced, or whether emergency calls are answered. The AI has nothing specific to quote, so it quotes a competitor instead.
- **An unclaimed or thin Google Business Profile.** No hours, no service list, no recent photos. This is the single most common reason a real, established company does not get named.
- **Zero structured data.** Without schema, a machine has to infer what the business does from marketing copy. Inference is where you lose to the company that spelled it out.
- **Stale reviews.** A burst of reviews from two years ago followed by silence reads as a business that has gone quiet. Recency signals that you are active and worth recommending.
- **Inconsistent listings.** The phone number on the website does not match Yelp, which does not match the old directory listing. Each mismatch chips away at the AI's confidence in naming you.

Fix these five and you are ahead of most of your market, because most of your competitors have not. The contractors winning AI search today are rarely the biggest. They are the ones who made their business the easiest to understand.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I really influence what ChatGPT says about my business?

Yes. ChatGPT and other assistants build their answers from public information about your business: your website, Google Business Profile, reviews, and mentions across the web. Improve the clarity and consistency of those signals and you improve the odds of being named.

### Does ChatGPT use Google reviews?

Indirectly, yes. AI assistants weigh reputation signals, and a strong, recent base of reviews on Google and trade directories makes a business look more active and trustworthy. Volume, recency, and your responses all matter.

### What is AEO (answer engine optimization)?

AEO is the practice of optimizing your business so AI answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini name and recommend you. It overlaps with SEO but puts more weight on clarity, structured data, and trust signals than on keywords and backlinks alone.

### Do I need to optimize for ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini separately?

Mostly no. The foundations that get you named, a complete Google Business Profile, clear website copy, schema markup, and strong reviews, help across all major AI assistants at once. You do not need a separate strategy for each one.

### How is this different from regular SEO?

SEO aims to rank your pages in a list of links. AEO aims to get your business named inside an AI generated answer. SEO still matters, and good SEO helps your AEO, but an AI answer usually names only two or three companies, so the margin for being unclear is much smaller.

### How much does it cost to get recommended by AI?

The foundational work (profile, schema, reviews, consistent listings) is mostly time and attention rather than ad spend. Costs rise if you bring in help to build content, manage reviews, and maintain structured data, but you are not buying placement. AI assistants cannot be paid to recommend you.

### Will running Google or Facebook ads get me recommended by ChatGPT?

No. AI assistants cannot be paid for placement, and ad spend does not buy you a spot in their answers. Ads can drive traffic and reviews, which indirectly strengthens your reputation signals over time, but the recommendation itself is earned through clarity, consistency, and trust. Any service that promises to buy your way into AI answers is selling something that does not exist.
