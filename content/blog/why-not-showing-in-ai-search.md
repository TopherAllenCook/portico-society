Your business is not showing up in AI search because the assistants cannot find clear, consistent, trusted information about it. ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini build answers from your Google Business Profile, website, reviews, and structured data. When those signals are thin, inconsistent, or missing, the AI names a competitor it understands better instead.

## TL;DR: Key Takeaways

- AI assistants name businesses they can clearly understand and verify, not the biggest spenders.
- The usual culprits: a thin Google Business Profile, a vague website, no schema markup, stale reviews, and inconsistent listings.
- About 60 percent of home service searches now start with an AI tool ([BrightLocal, 2025](https://www.brightlocal.com)), so being invisible there costs real jobs.
- Most fixes are free and within your control, and the foundation work registers within weeks.
- Start with your Google Business Profile and your website's clarity. They move the needle fastest.

## Why do homeowners use AI to find contractors now?

A homeowner with a problem used to open Google and scan a list of links. A growing share now open ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini and ask directly: "who is the best plumber near me," or "which HVAC company should I call." The assistant replies with two or three names, and the homeowner calls one.

This matters because the AI answer is short. Where a search results page lists ten options, an AI answer usually names three. If you are not one of them, you are not losing the comparison. You were never in it, and you never see the job.

## Why isn't my business showing up in AI search?

If an assistant is not naming your company, it is almost always one of these reasons. Most established businesses have several of them at once.

- **A thin or unclaimed Google Business Profile.** This is the most common cause. No complete service list, no hours, no recent photos.
- **A vague website.** The site never states your trade, your service area, or whether you take emergency calls in plain language the AI can quote.
- **No structured data (schema).** Without it, the AI has to guess what your business does instead of reading it directly.
- **Stale or thin reviews.** A handful of old reviews signals a business that has gone quiet.
- **Inconsistent listings.** Your name, address, and phone number do not match across your site, Google, and directories, which lowers the AI's confidence.

## How do AI assistants decide which businesses to name?

Assistants synthesize answers from the information they can find and trust. They lean on a few sources: your Google Business Profile, your website, your reviews, structured data, and mentions of your business across the web. The businesses that get named consistently make all of those signals clear and consistent.

Here is what an assistant effectively checks, and how most invisible businesses score.

| Signal | What the AI wants | Why businesses miss it |
|---|---|---|
| Google Business Profile | Complete, accurate, recent | Half-filled or unclaimed |
| Website clarity | Trade, area, services stated plainly | Buried in vague marketing copy |
| Structured data | Schema declaring services and area | Absent on most contractor sites |
| Reviews | Recent, steady, responded to | Old and sparse |
| Consistency | Matching info everywhere | Mismatched phone numbers and names |

## How do I get my business to show up in AI search?

You do not need a big budget. You need to set the foundation. Work these in order of speed-to-impact.

1. **Complete your Google Business Profile.** Claim it, fill every field, list all services, set your area and hours, and add real photos. This is the highest-impact step.
2. **Make your website answer the obvious questions.** State your trade, cities, brands serviced, and emergency availability in plain language.
3. **Add schema markup.** Declare your business type, services, and service area so machines do not have to guess.
4. **Build steady reviews.** Ask after every job, respond to each, and keep it consistent.
5. **Fix your listings.** Make your name, address, and phone identical everywhere.

## How long until my business shows up in AI search?

It depends on where you start. Profile and schema fixes can register within a few weeks. Reviews and external mentions compound over one to three months. The honest framing is that foundation work pays back fastest and authority work builds over time. The businesses being named today started months ago, which is the argument for starting now.

## What keeps most businesses invisible in AI search?

The pattern is rarely a single mistake. It is a foundation that was never set, because the owner is great at the work and never had a reason to think about machine-readable signals. The good news is the same: most of your competitors have not set it either, so the bar to become one of the named few is lower than it looks.

## What does AI search look like for a contractor?

Picture a homeowner whose water heater just failed. Instead of searching Google and scanning a list, they open ChatGPT and type, "my water heater is leaking, who is a good plumber in Dallas." The assistant does not return ten links. It returns a short paragraph that names two or three companies, often with a sentence about why each one fits. The homeowner reads it, picks a name, and calls. The entire decision happens inside the answer, before any traditional search result loads.

That is the shift in one scene. The list of links still exists below, but a growing number of homeowners never scroll to it. They trust the assistant's shortlist the way they once trusted a neighbor's referral. If your business is not in that shortlist, the homeowner forms an impression of who the good plumbers in town are, and you are simply not part of it.

## How is AI search different from the Google map pack?

The Google map pack, the three local listings shown with the map, is still important, but it works differently from an AI answer. The map pack is driven heavily by proximity and shows clickable options the homeowner chooses among. An AI answer is driven by how clearly the assistant understands and trusts each business, and it makes much of the choice for the homeowner by naming only a few.

That difference changes where you invest. To win the map pack, proximity and reviews carry a lot of weight. To win the AI answer, clarity and consistency carry more, because the assistant has to be confident enough to put your name in a short, declarative recommendation. The overlap is large, since a complete profile and strong reviews help both, but the AI answer punishes vagueness more, because there is no list to fall back into.

## Which trades does AI search affect most?

Every local service trade is affected, but the impact is largest where the job is urgent and valuable. Plumbing, HVAC, electrical, and roofing all fit: a homeowner with a burst pipe, a dead furnace, a tripped panel, or a leaking roof wants help immediately and trusts the first credible name the assistant gives. In those moments, being named is close to being chosen.

Lower-urgency trades still benefit, but the window is less winner-take-all. The practical takeaway is the same across trades: the businesses that make themselves easy to understand and verify get named, and the ones that stay vague get skipped, regardless of how good their actual work is.

## What happens to the businesses that get this right?

The businesses that set these foundations do not just appear in AI answers once. They compound. Each accurate listing, each recent review, and each piece of clear content gives the assistants more evidence, which makes them more confident naming you, which earns more calls, which produces more reviews. The advantage builds on itself.

The reverse is also true. A business that stays invisible gives the assistants no reason to change their answer, so the same competitors get named month after month while the invisible business never learns about the jobs it lost. The gap is quiet, but it widens. Starting now is how you get on the right side of that compounding.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why is my competitor showing up in AI search and not me?

Usually because their information is clearer and more consistent than yours, not because they spend more. A complete Google Business Profile, plain website copy, schema, and steady reviews make a business easy for an assistant to name. Close those gaps and you compete for the same spot.

### Can I pay to show up in AI search?

No. AI assistants cannot be paid for placement in their answers. Visibility is earned through clarity, consistency, reviews, and trust signals. Any service that promises to buy your way into AI answers is selling something that does not exist.

### Does my website matter for AI search?

Yes. Assistants read your website to understand what you do, where, and for whom. A clear site that plainly states your trade, service area, and services gives the AI specific facts to quote. A vague brochure site gives it nothing.

### Do reviews affect AI search visibility?

Yes, indirectly. A recent, steady flow of reviews signals that your business is active and trusted, which makes an assistant more comfortable recommending you. Volume, recency, and your responses all contribute.

### Which AI assistants should I focus on?

You do not need a separate strategy for each. The same foundations, a complete profile, clear website, schema, and strong reviews, help across ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and others at once.

### How do I check if I show up in AI search?

Ask the assistants directly. Type "best [your trade] in [your city]" into ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini and see if your name appears. A free audit can do this for you and rank the gaps to fix.

### Does my website need to mention my city to show up in AI search?

Yes, naming the cities and areas you serve helps significantly. AI assistants answer location-specific questions like "best plumber in Austin," so a website that plainly states its service area gives the assistant the geographic match it needs. List your city and the surrounding towns you cover in plain text, rather than buried in an image or a map, so an assistant can easily connect your business to a local search.
