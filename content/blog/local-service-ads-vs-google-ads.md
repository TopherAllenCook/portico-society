Local Service Ads vs Google Ads comes down to how you pay and how much control you want: Local Service Ads charge per lead and sit at the very top with a Google Guaranteed badge, while Google Ads charge per click and give you full control of your message and landing pages. LSA leads are usually cheaper, and Google Ads win on targeting. Most contractors should run both.

## TL;DR: Key Takeaways

- **Local Service Ads (LSA)** are pay-per-lead, appear above everything else, and carry the Google Guaranteed badge. Leads run roughly 40 to 65 dollars ([Searchlight Digital, 2026](https://searchlightdigital.io/google-local-service-ads-cost-per-lead/)).
- **Google Ads (search)** are pay-per-click, sit just below LSA, and give you control over keywords, copy, and landing pages. Leads average around 90 dollars and climb higher for urgent jobs ([Hook Agency](https://hookagency.com/blog/google-ads-vs-local-service-ads-home-services/)).
- LSA is usually the cheaper, faster path to booked calls. Google Ads wins on control, targeting, and brand.
- Most established contractors should run both: LSA to capture the cheapest high-intent calls, Google Ads to control the message and catch everything LSA misses.
- The metric that decides it is cost per booked job, not cost per lead.

## What are Local Service Ads?

Local Service Ads (LSA) are Google's pay-per-lead listings for local service businesses. They appear at the very top of the search results, above the regular Google Ads, and show your business name, rating, and a Google Guaranteed badge. To run them you pass a verification and background check, which is what earns the badge.

The defining feature is the pricing model: you pay when a homeowner contacts you, not when they click. A lead is usually a phone call or message, so you are paying for someone who actually wanted to reach a contractor, not just a click.

## What are Google Ads for contractors?

Google Ads (the search ads) are pay-per-click text ads that appear just below the Local Service Ads. You bid on keywords like "emergency plumber" or "AC repair near me," write the ad copy, and send clicks to a landing page you control.

The defining feature here is control. You decide the exact search terms, the message, the offer, the landing page, and the extensions. The tradeoff is that you pay for every click whether or not it becomes a lead, and the average home services cost per click is about 7.85 dollars ([LocalIQ, 2025](https://localiq.com/blog/home-services-search-advertising-benchmarks/)).

## Local Service Ads vs Google Ads: the key differences

Here is how the two stack up for a home service business.

| Factor | Local Service Ads | Google Ads (search) |
|---|---|---|
| You pay for | A lead (call or message) | A click |
| Position on the page | Very top | Just below LSA |
| Typical cost | $40 to $65 per lead | $90 to $150 per lead |
| Control of message | Low (Google sets the format) | High (your copy, landing page, offer) |
| Trust signal | Google Guaranteed badge | None |
| Setup | License and background verification | Account, keywords, campaigns |
| Best at | Cheap, high-intent booked calls | Targeting, messaging, and brand control |

The pattern: LSA buys you cheap, ready-to-call leads with little effort and little control. Google Ads costs more per lead but lets you control exactly who sees what, and where they land.

## Which is cheaper, Local Service Ads or Google Ads?

Local Service Ads are usually cheaper per lead. Reported averages put LSA leads around 40 to 65 dollars by trade, while Google Ads search leads average closer to 90 dollars and climb past 150 for emergency and high-value work.

But cheaper per lead is not the same as cheaper per job. LSA leads are sometimes lower intent or land outside your ideal service area, and Google Ads sends traffic to a page you control, which can close at a higher rate. Always carry the comparison through to cost per booked job. ## When should a contractor use Local Service Ads?

Use Local Service Ads when you want booked calls fast with minimal setup. They fit best when:

- You want the cheapest high-intent leads and the top-of-page Google Guaranteed badge.
- You can answer the phone quickly, because LSA leads are often calling right now.
- You serve a clear, well-defined area and set of services.
- You want a low-effort channel that runs without constant management.

## When should a contractor use Google Ads?

Use Google Ads when control and targeting matter. They fit best when:

- You want to own the message, the offer, and the landing page experience.
- You are promoting a specific service, season, or promotion.
- You want to capture searches LSA does not cover, or appear when a competitor is searched.
- You are building brand recognition that lowers your cost per lead over time.

## Should you run both Local Service Ads and Google Ads?

For most established contractors, yes. The two are complementary, not competing. Local Service Ads grab the cheapest, highest-intent calls at the top of the page. Google Ads then capture the homeowners who scroll past LSA, who want more detail before calling, or who are searching terms LSA does not serve.

Running both also gives you more of the page. When your business shows up in the LSA block and again in the paid results, you take up more of the homeowner's attention and crowd out competitors. The budget split depends on your market, but starting with LSA and layering Google Ads on top is the common, effective sequence.

## How should a contractor decide where to start?

If your budget is tight and you need booked jobs quickly, start with Local Service Ads. They are the lowest-effort, lowest-cost-per-lead way to get the phone ringing, and the Google Guaranteed badge builds instant trust.

Once LSA is producing and you want more volume or more control, add Google Ads. Use them to target the specific services and searches that matter most, send traffic to strong landing pages, and capture demand LSA leaves on the table. The goal over time is a mix you control, alongside the owned demand that lowers your blended cost per lead.

## What mistakes do contractors make with LSA and Google Ads?

- **Judging on cost per lead, not cost per booked job.** A cheap LSA lead that never books costs more than a pricier Google Ads lead that closes.
- **Sending Google Ads clicks to the homepage.** Clicks should land on a focused page about the exact service, or they leak away.
- **Ignoring LSA disputes.** You can dispute clearly invalid LSA leads, but many contractors never do and overpay.
- **Slow response.** Both channels reward speed. The first contractor to answer usually wins the job.
- **Running only one when both would compound.** Relying on a single channel leaves easy jobs on the table and gives you no fallback.

## How much should you budget for each?

There is no universal split, but a practical starting point helps. If you are new to both, put the majority of your paid budget into Local Service Ads first, since they produce the cheapest booked calls with the least setup. Once LSA is consistently delivering and you know your cost per booked job, add Google Ads with a smaller test budget aimed at your highest-value services.

From there, let the numbers move the money. Shift budget toward whichever channel is booking jobs at the lower cost per booked job in your market, and keep enough in each to hold your position on the page. In competitive metros, many contractors run both at meaningful budgets because the page is crowded and owning two slots matters. In quieter markets, Local Service Ads alone may carry most of the load.

## Where does owned demand fit alongside paid ads?

Paid ads, whether Local Service Ads or Google Ads, stop the moment you stop paying. That makes them a faucet, not a foundation. The contractors who win long term pair paid ads with owned demand: a strong Google Business Profile, visibility in AI search, a website that converts, and a steady flow of reviews. Owned demand carries no per-lead fee, so every lead it produces pulls your blended cost per lead down.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are Local Service Ads worth it for contractors?

For most established home service businesses, yes. They deliver high-intent, pay-per-lead calls at the very top of the page with a Google Guaranteed badge, usually at a lower cost per lead than Google Ads. They are worth it as long as you answer quickly and track your cost per booked job.

### Is LSA cheaper than Google Ads?

Usually, yes. Local Service Ads commonly cost 40 to 65 dollars per lead, while Google Ads search leads average around 90 dollars and more for urgent work. LSA also charges per lead instead of per click, though you give up control over the message.

### What is the Google Guaranteed badge?

It is a green checkmark badge Google adds to Local Service Ads after a business passes license and background verification. It signals trust and can qualify eligible jobs for the Google Guarantee, which may reimburse a customer up to a limit if they are unsatisfied, subject to Google's terms.

### Do Local Service Ads or Google Ads show up first?

Local Service Ads appear first. They sit at the very top of the results, above the regular Google Ads, which appear just below them. Organic results and the map pack appear lower still.

### Can I run Local Service Ads and Google Ads at the same time?

Yes, and most contractors should. They are separate products with separate budgets, and running both lets you take the top LSA slot and a paid search slot at once, capturing more of the page and more homeowners.

### Which is better for emergency services?

Local Service Ads are often ideal for emergencies because the leads are calls from people who need help now, and the badge builds instant trust. Google Ads complement them by letting you target specific emergency searches and control the landing page. Running both covers the most ground.

### Do I need a big budget to start with Local Service Ads?

No. You can start Local Service Ads with a modest weekly budget and scale up as the leads prove out. Because you pay per lead rather than per click, a smaller budget still buys real, high-intent calls. Many contractors begin with LSA precisely because it produces booked jobs without a large upfront commitment.

### How much do Local Service Ads cost?

Costs vary by trade and market, but Local Service Ads leads commonly run about 40 to 65 dollars each. You set a budget and pay per lead, and you can dispute clearly invalid leads to control spend.
