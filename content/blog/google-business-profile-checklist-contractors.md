Your Google Business Profile is the single highest-leverage free marketing asset a contractor has. It drives your local ranking, your map pack visibility, and increasingly whether AI assistants name you at all. This Google Business Profile checklist walks through every field and signal that matters, in the order that moves the needle fastest.

## TL;DR: Key Takeaways

- A complete, accurate Google Business Profile is the fastest free way to win more local and AI visibility.
- The essentials: claim it, fill every field, list all services, set categories, add real photos, and keep your name, address, and phone consistent everywhere.
- Reviews are part of the profile. Ask after every job and respond to each one.
- Your profile feeds both Google's local results and AI assistants, so a half-filled profile costs you twice.
- Most contractors have an incomplete profile, which means completing yours is an easy way to pull ahead.

## Why does a Google Business Profile matter so much for contractors?

When a homeowner searches for a contractor, the map pack, those three local listings with the map, sits near the top of the results and gets a large share of the clicks and calls. Your Google Business Profile is what determines whether you appear there. It is free, it is trusted, and it is where many homeowners decide who to call.

It now matters for a second reason. AI assistants lean heavily on Google Business Profiles because the data is structured and verified. A complete profile helps you rank locally and helps you get named when a homeowner asks an assistant for a recommendation. A weak profile costs you in both places at once.

## The Google Business Profile checklist

Work through these in order. The early items carry the most weight.

1. **Claim and verify the profile.** If you have not claimed it, do that first. An unverified profile cannot be fully optimized.
2. **Set your primary category precisely.** Choose the most specific category for your trade (for example, "HVAC contractor," not just "contractor"), then add relevant secondary categories.
3. **Complete every field.** Business name, address, phone, website, hours, and service area. Leave nothing blank.
4. **List all your services.** Add each service you offer with a short description. This is what Google and AI match against homeowner searches.
5. **Write a clear business description.** State plainly what you do, where, and for whom. Avoid filler and keep it specific.
6. **Add real photos.** Upload genuine photos of your team, trucks, and completed work. Profiles with real photos earn more trust and engagement than stock-only listings.
7. **Set accurate hours, including holidays.** Wrong hours cost you calls and erode trust.
8. **Turn on messaging if you can respond fast.** Only enable it if someone will reply quickly, since slow replies hurt more than no messaging.
9. **Keep your name, address, and phone identical everywhere.** Your profile, website, and every directory must match exactly.
10. **Build and respond to reviews.** Ask after every completed job and respond to each review, positive or negative.

## How do you choose the right Google Business Profile categories?

Categories are one of the most underused parts of a profile, and getting them right is one of the fastest wins. Your primary category should be the most specific match for your core trade. An HVAC company should choose "HVAC contractor" rather than the broader "contractor," because the specific category tells Google and AI assistants exactly what you do and matches you to the searches that count. Then add secondary categories for the other services you genuinely offer, such as "furnace repair service" or "air conditioning repair service."

Resist the urge to add categories you do not really serve in hopes of catching more searches. It dilutes the signal and can attract the wrong inquiries. Precise categories beat broad ones every time, because both Google and the assistants are trying to match a homeowner's specific need to the business that clearly handles it.

## What makes Google Business Profile photos actually work?

Photos are not decoration on a profile, they are evidence. Genuine images of your team, your trucks, and your finished work tell a homeowner that you are real, local, and capable in a way that words cannot. Profiles with authentic, recent photos tend to earn more views and more calls than listings that rely on a logo and a stock image or two.

The key word is authentic. A homeowner can tell the difference between a real photo of a crew on a roof and a generic stock image, and so, increasingly, can the systems ranking you. Add new photos regularly rather than uploading a batch once and forgetting the profile. A steady stream of real images signals an active, trustworthy business.

## How do reviews fit into your Google Business Profile?

Reviews are part of your profile, not a separate project, and they carry enormous weight. They influence your local ranking, they shape whether a homeowner calls after they find you, and they feed the trust AI assistants rely on when deciding whether to name you. A business with a steady stream of recent, responded-to reviews looks active and dependable. One with a dozen old reviews and no responses looks like it has gone quiet.

The habit that matters is simple: ask every satisfied customer right after the job, make it easy with a direct link, and respond to every review you receive, good or bad. Responding to a hard review calmly and professionally often impresses future readers more than a wall of perfect ratings. Consistency over months beats any one-time push.

## Why does consistency across listings matter so much?

Your Google Business Profile does not exist in isolation. Homeowners and machines also see your business on your website, on Yelp, in trade directories, and in old listings you may have forgotten about. When the name, address, and phone number match exactly everywhere, you look like one solid, established business. When they conflict, even in small ways, you introduce doubt.

That doubt has a cost. Conflicting information makes Google less certain about your local relevance and makes AI assistants less confident naming you. Auditing your listings for consistency is unglamorous work, but it is some of the highest-return cleanup a contractor can do, because it quietly raises the trust behind every other signal.

## How do you turn a strong profile into booked jobs?

A complete profile earns visibility, but visibility only pays when it converts. Make sure the path from your profile to a booked job is short and obvious. Your phone number should be correct and answered, your booking link or website should load fast and make it easy to request service, and your hours should be accurate so a homeowner who calls actually reaches you. The best profile in the city still loses the job if the call goes to voicemail.

Treat the profile as the top of a funnel that ends in a scheduled appointment: visibility, then trust, then an easy way to act, then a fast response. When all four are in place, your Google Business Profile stops being a listing and starts being one of your most reliable sources of work.

## How often should you update your Google Business Profile?

Treat it as a living asset, not a one-time setup. Post updates, add new photos, refresh your services as they change, and respond to reviews as they come in. A steady cadence signals to Google and to AI assistants that the business is active. Quarterly is a reasonable minimum, and monthly is better if you can manage it.

## What mistakes do contractors make with Google Business Profiles?

- **Leaving fields blank.** Every empty field is a missed match against a homeowner's search.
- **Vague categories.** A generic category buries you under more specific competitors.
- **Stock-only photos.** Real photos of real work build trust that stock images cannot.
- **Inconsistent contact info.** A phone number that does not match your website lowers trust with both Google and AI.
- **Ignoring reviews.** Not asking for reviews, or never responding, leaves your most powerful trust signal weak.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is a Google Business Profile free for contractors?

Yes. Creating, claiming, and optimizing a Google Business Profile is completely free. It is the highest-return free marketing asset most contractors have, since it drives local visibility, map pack placement, and AI recommendations without any ad spend.

### How do I rank higher in the Google map pack?

Completeness, relevance, proximity, and reviews drive map pack ranking. Fully complete your profile, set precise categories and services, keep your information consistent everywhere, and build a steady flow of recent reviews. Proximity to the searcher also matters and is outside your control.

### Does my Google Business Profile help me show up in ChatGPT?

Yes, indirectly. AI assistants treat your profile as a trusted, structured source about your business. A complete profile with clear services, categories, and strong reviews makes assistants more likely to name you when a homeowner asks for a recommendation.

### How many photos should I add to my Google Business Profile?

There is no hard limit worth chasing, but add enough genuine photos to show your team, vehicles, and completed work, and keep adding new ones over time. Real, recent photos build more trust and engagement than a handful of stock images.

### How do I get more Google reviews?

Ask every satisfied customer right after the job, make it easy with a direct review link, and respond to the reviews you receive. A steady, recent flow matters more than a one-time burst, and responding shows both customers and Google that you are engaged.

### Should I list every service on my profile?

Yes. List each service you offer with a short description. The services section is one of the main things Google and AI assistants match against homeowner searches, so leaving services off means missing the searches that match them.

### Can I manage my Google Business Profile myself, or do I need an agency?

You can absolutely manage it yourself. Claiming the profile, completing the fields, adding photos, and asking for reviews are all things a motivated owner can do without help. Where many businesses bring in support is keeping it consistent over time: posting updates, maintaining listings across the web, responding to every review, and tying the profile into the rest of their marketing. If you have the time and discipline, do it yourself. If you would rather it be done right and kept current, that is the case for help.
