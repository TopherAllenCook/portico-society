## 2. The full funnel pipeline (his 8-stage system)

**The strategic premise (why value-first works) — verified.** Suby segments any market by buying-readiness: only **~3% are ready to buy now**; the other **97%** aren't (≈60% problem-unaware, 20% problem-aware, 17% information-gathering). Selling hard only fights over the 3%; giving value first captures the 97% and warms them. "Most businesses are terrible at selling because they try to close deals too early." "The person who gives the most value upfront wins." (Note: this is Suby's rebrand of Chet Holmes' Buyers Pyramid / Eugene Schwartz's awareness stages — the 60/20/17 split is his formulation, the idea isn't original to him. That's fine; you're copying the *application*.)

This is the spine. From his own framing (videos + Facebook posts), the system is:

1. **Identify your dream buyer** — the Halo Strategy (§3).
2. **Craft the bait** — a High-Value Content Offer (HVCO): a free report, tool, or book that gives away the secrets. "Make your free stuff better than your competitor's paid stuff."
3. **Win the attention war / buy traffic** — the ad's only job is to **sell the click**, not the product. "You can't buy off a Facebook ad."
4. **Capture leads** — opt-in for the bait (name + email, only the data you need).
5. **Nurture with content** — indoctrinate. Take them "from 'who the hell are you' to 'shut up and take my money.'"
6. **The Magic Lantern Technique** — the pre-sell engine (§ below).
7. **Sales conversion** — the Godfather Offer (§4), delivered via VSL, application, and/or a strategy call.
8. **Automate and multiply** — systematize the winners, then scale spend.

**The Magic Lantern Technique (stage 6) — verbatim mechanic:** "Call out your audience that are experiencing a problem, but instead of saying buy my stuff, you are then educating them about all the things about the problem... all the existing solutions in the marketplace, and how they fall short. And then you pivot into your offer." Rule: everything before the pivot must be pure value — "regardless of if someone watches the entire video sales letter and they don't buy, they still walk away with value." This *is* the "helping hand" move, mechanized.

## 3. The Halo Strategy (dream-buyer research)

**Important correction:** in his *own* videos the Halo Strategy is **not** a four-phase acronym (no "HEAR/HELP"). Third-party blogs invented that. In his words it is a **dream-buyer research-and-avatar method** — a fill-in template you complete by mining where your market actually talks:

1. **Start with your best clients (80/20).** Profile the 20% that bring 80% of profit and cash flow.
2. **Go past demographics into psychographics** — "what's keeping them up at night... things they don't tell anybody... they don't even tell their spouse."
3. **Forum-forage and copy their exact language verbatim.** "Reddit, Amazon reviews, YouTube comments, blogs, forums... cut and copy those things into your document." Do **not** rewrite in your own words — steal their literal vernacular.
4. **Use multiple sources** because intent differs (free-content engagement ≠ what people say after they've "parted with their hard-earned cash").
5. **Map their world of influence** — the TV/Netflix shows they watch, where they hang out, "so you can serve them, not just sell them."
6. **Build the avatar from data, never imagination.** His contrarian jab: most people "sit back with a cup of coffee... her name is Sally, she's got three kids... they're just making that stuff up."
7. **Reference the avatar before writing anything** — every ad, email, VSL.

Payoff: "You will have enough fodder to create endless ads... your words strike like lightning." Refresh quarterly, no less than every 6 months.

## 4. The Godfather Offer (the 7 components)

"So white hot, so irresistible that your prospects can't refuse it and only a lunatic would refuse." "If the offer doesn't keep the founder up at night, then it simply isn't strong enough."

**Pre-work:** Hit the **bullseye** ("the center of the center of what people really want"). Build a **features→benefits list**. Sell what the market is *starving for*, not what you happen to have. Start from "if you had a magic wand" and wind back to what you can actually deliver. Then **burden the risk yourself**.

**The 7 components (verbatim order):**

1. **Rationale** — a credible *why* the deal exists, to silence the "why are they doing this?" voice. ("We accidentally over-ordered inventory.")
2. **Build value** — state the RRP, itemize every component, assign each a dollar value, then show the price as "a pittance of the real value." Even free consults get a stated value.
3. **Pricing** — value-based, not cost-based. "Charge just 10% of the total value so it's an absolute no-brainer." "You know you've got a good offer when your prospect is trying to justify how you can even make money doing it."
4. **Payment plans** — "people value today-money more than tomorrow-money." Split it.
5. **Premiums (bonuses)** — stack 3–4. "Give away all your secrets and sell the implementation. Information is abundant." (Physical premiums work great.)
6. **Power guarantee** — reverse the risk hard. "Double your money back." Redemption is usually <3%. "If it's by law and you'd already do it, why not advertise it?"
7. **Scarcity** — must be **real**, never fake. "People find that stuff out very quickly." "Delay is the death of a sale."

**What a Godfather Offer is NOT:** "great customer service... outstanding quality... in business since 1982. No one gives a damn about any of that."

> **On the component count:** the 7 above are from his own Godfather Offer video (primary). Book-summary sources compress it to a verified **5-element build** — (1) solve the #1 pain, (2) bold risk-reversal guarantee, (3) bonuses, (4) scarcity, (5) value-stack (list component values, then reveal a lower price). Various blogs publish "8-step" and "9-component" versions; those specific numbered lists did **not** survive verification, so don't treat any single numbering as canon. The *elements* are stable; the count is just packaging. Build from the elements.

## 5. Headlines: the tabloid method + the formulas + the "generator" tool

This is the part you specifically love. Here's exactly how he does it.

### 5a. Sourcing — he harvests from where attention is brutally A/B-tested

> "Study where attention and money flows: **TMZ, the New York Times, Lad Bible, BuzzFeed.**" Words that recur: **"exposed, shocked, leak, wild details emerge,"** plus numbers. "We are all monkeys in disguise."

The deeper principle: **supermarket-checkout tabloids** (National Enquirer, Cosmopolitan, Men's/Women's Health, Weekly World News) are the most ruthlessly optimized headlines on earth — they have *no brand equity to coast on* and must sell off the rack to a distracted stranger in 2 seconds, every single issue, or the publisher dies. So their headline structures are pre-validated by decades of survival. The method:

- **Keep a swipe file.** Photograph/clip every headline that stops you. Tag the structure (curiosity, threat, number, contrast, social proof).
- **Re-skin proven structures for your market** — don't reinvent, transplant. His signature proof: a 1982 book selling ~2,000 copies was **re-titled** (nothing inside changed) to *"How to Satisfy a Woman Every Time and Have Her Beg for More"* → **2.3 million copies in 18 months.** "Same book, same content, just different packaging."
- **Ogilvy's law:** "On average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy... once you've written your headline, you've spent 80 cents of your dollar." So spend 80% of your time on it.

### 5b. The formulas (rip-and-run)

**The 4 timeless formulas (from the 93-min copywriting video):**
1. *"X ways to [achieve desirable thing] without [undesirable thing]."* → "Six ways to get washboard abs without doing a single sit-up."
2. *"[Do difficult thing] in [specific time], even if [shortcoming]."* → "Pay off your mortgage in 7 years even if you have a modest income."
3. *"[Achieve thing] like [an expert], even without [the thing expected]."* → "Paint a masterpiece like Picasso without ever having a single lesson."
4. *"How to eliminate [biggest problem] without [thing they hate] within [time frame]."* → "How to eliminate your muffin top without giving up the foods you love within 21 days."

**The "Ultimate" formula (King Kong blog):** Number + Adjective + Keyword + Rationale + Promise. E.g. "How to Generate 100 Leads in Under 5 Minutes Using Facebook."

**The S.H.I.N.E. test** (run on any draft): **S**pecific, **H**elpful, **I**mmediacy, **N**ewsworthy, **E**ntertaining.

**The 5 headline essentials:** (1) grab with 3–4 words, don't tell the whole story ("grab your qualified prospect by the throat"); (2) always use numbers; (3) burning curiosity + a qualifier twist; (4) clear what's-in-it-for-them benefit; (5) be specific.

**Most-shared headline words (ranked):** List posts → You/Your → Free/Giveaway → How to → DIY → I/Me/My → Easy → Win → New → Ways → Why → Video → The Most.

### 5c. The "headline generator tool"

What he sells today is **Kong (getkong.ai)** — an AI copy tool "trained by $7.8 billion ROAS / $200m in ad spend" on his winning swipe DNA. It generates ads, headlines, and funnels, carries a "20% ROAS boost or it's free" guarantee, and is priced Free 7-day → Pro $79/mo → Agency $299/mo. Note the *double duty*: the tool is **both a useful utility and a lead-gen mechanism** that ascends users toward the agency. (He also references the **EMV Headline Analyzer** as an emotional-value scoring tool in the blog.) The underlying human method is still 5a: feed the AI your best swipe so it generates from "the best DNA, the best germs" — you stay the **"copy chief"** over the machine.

The register: **confident, profane, contrarian, proof-obsessed, and relentlessly customer-centric.** Profanity functions as *credibility signaling* ("I'm a real operator, not a corporate marketer"). But note the recurring ethical caveat — "ethical bribe," "be honest," "this isn't fake scarcity" — the edge is paired with an honesty claim. That pairing is the whole "caught-you/helping-hand" personality.

### 6b. The 10 core copy principles (93-min video)

1. **80% prospect / 20% you.** Write about "what's keeping them up at 3am in a cold sweat."
2. **Features tell, benefits sell.** Sell "dropping dress sizes," not "12 weeks of 6am HIIT."
3. **"Under the fingernail" specificity** — so specific they can't deny it.
4. **What you say > how you say it** — "you can't outwrite bad research."
5. **Channel existing desire** (Collier: "enter the conversation already in their mind").
6. **6th-grade reading level** (Hemingway app). "Consumption precedes conversion."
7. **Long-form beats short-form**, but "every word must fight for its right to be on the page. Write for the buyers, not the receptionist."
8. **Value is the antidote to skepticism** — give away the good stuff.
9. **Each funnel step has ONE job** — the ad sells the click.
10. **Be the copy chief over AI** — own the big idea; feed AI your winners.

### 6c. The 17-step sales structure (the skeleton of any ad / VSL / sales page)

Call out the audience (eyebrow) → benefit headline → back it up in the subhead → create intrigue → floodlight the pain ("kick the bruised knee") → unique solution (dismantle alternatives) → credentials → benefits → social proof → irresistible offer → 3–4 sweeteners/bonuses → stack the value → reveal price → real scarcity → power guarantee → CTA as an embedded command ("don't pussyfoot around it") → P.S. ("lead with a negative, end with a positive").

### 6d. The 60 power words (build your own list)

- **Fear:** failure, danger, humiliation, vulnerable, mistake, warning, devastating, risk.
- **Urgency:** now, hurry, limited, running out, deadline, last chance, expires, never again.
- **Exclusivity:** members only, request an invitation, become an insider, be the first, login required.
- **Trust/risk-reversal:** guarantee, refund, certified, no obligation, proven, no risk, unconditional, money back.
- **Temptation:** secret, confidential, forbidden, controversial, insider.
- **Personal:** **You** and **Because** (the Cialdini Xerox study — 94% compliance when you give a reason).

### 6e. Email subject-line formulas

Curiosity ("Did you see what happened to this guy's face? (VIDEO)") · prediction · news · quote a B.U.D.A. (Big Undeniable Dominant Authority) · invitation · free gift · flattery · benefit · question · statistic ("5,123 people lost 10 pounds using this trick").

### 6f. The 5 questions every buyer is silently asking

Will I love it (love)? · Will I miss out (FOMO)? · Am I a bad person if I don't (guilt)? · Will I be proud (pride)? · What's the advantage for me (greed)? Answer all five somewhere on the page.

## 8. Offers, pricing, and sales mechanics

- **Front-end CTA = free, high-value, value-anchored.** The agency's front-end conversion offer is a **free 30-minute "Scaling Growth Map" call, anchored at "$1,000 value"** (verbatim from kingkong.co/30-minute-strategy-session: *"Claim your 100% FREE no-obligation 30-minute scaling growth map call ($1,000 value)."*). Putting a price on the free thing is straight from the Godfather doctrine (component 2: "you need to put a value attached to what it would cost if it wasn't free").
- **Application-gated by a 15+ screen qualifying form** *before* the prospect ever reaches a calendar. The actual questions: What do you sell (product/service)? · What marketing are you running now (FB/Google/SEO/TV/radio/print/none)? · Monthly marketing budget (under $5k → $50k+)? · Current monthly revenue? · Target monthly revenue? · Your #1 biggest obstacle? · When do you want to start? · **"On a scale of 1–10... 1 = I'm fine where I am, 10 = I'll do anything... what number are you?"** Then: "Fill out your details and you'll be taken to a calendar on the next page." This both qualifies leads and makes the prospect *invest effort* (commitment/consistency) before the call.
- **The disqualification / takeaway as scarcity (verbatim, swipe-file p.23):** *"We can't help everyone. This offer is for legitimate and hungry DTC brands who are doing at least $50,000 per month, who are already running ads and are looking to scale FAST... This isn't for brand-new beginners, dropshippers, broke business owners, or B2B E-commerce businesses. No hard feelings."* Telling people who it's *not* for raises desire in those it *is* for.
- **The two-path close (verbatim, swipe-file p.22) — this IS your "caught you / helping hand" template:** *"before you apply to work with us, we should get to know each other first... a free 30-minute growth map scaling call... 1- You can implement that plan on your own, or... 2- You can partner with us... There's no obligation to work with us, or for us to work with you, whatsoever."* Note the move: he explicitly gives them permission to take the value and leave. That generosity is what makes the partnership pitch land.
- **The flagship results guarantee (verbatim from kingkong.co homepage):** "**WE GUARANTEE TO BEAT YOUR RESULTS BY 30% OR WE WORK FOR FREE.**" (Conditions stated plainly: for businesses already running profitable ads; you cover ad spend; we control creative; apples-to-apples; 90 days.) This is the outrageous-guarantee doctrine applied: long enough to matter, conditions up front, no legalese. *(Skeptic note: third-party characterizations like "guaranteed results or you don't pay, <1% ever refund" could NOT be independently verified — rely on the exact homepage wording above, which is current as of June 2026.)*
- **Guarantee construction (4 steps):** know the customer → list ~10 purchase fears → counter 4–5 of them → make it outrageous (double-money-back, refund + compensation, pay for their time). Outrageous guarantees claim ~25% business lift with only 1–2% redemption.
- **Don't compete on price** — increase value (bonuses, services, customization, enhanced results, quality) and reverse risk.

## 9. THE RE-SKIN — your AI implementation agency

> The move you described: **hook them with something genuinely cool, then come in with the helping hand** — "we caught you this way, but here's who we really are; we just want to get you as much help as possible." That is *exactly* Sabri's Magic Lantern + HVCO model. Here's how to transplant every lever.

### 9a. The bait (your "cool free thing") — make it a TOOL, not a PDF

Sabri's data: **tool-based lead magnets beat info-based ones.** For an AI implementation agency, the bait should be a *working AI artifact they get to keep and feel*, so the "cool factor" is real, not promised. Options, strongest first:

1. **A free custom AI build / "we'll build you one automation free."** The ultimate "give away the secrets, sell the implementation." They feel the magic; you've already started the relationship.
2. **A free AI-powered audit** of their site/ops/funnel, *generated by your own AI in front of them* (mirror of the strategy session, but the tool does the wow).
3. **A self-serve mini-tool / custom GPT** they can use immediately (e.g., for a niche: "the [Industry] AI Quote Writer," "the [Industry] Lead-Reply Bot"). This is your Kong: utility + lead-gen in one.
4. **A swipe file of prompts/workflows** ("47 AI automations that replaced a $60k hire") — lowest effort, still tool-flavored.

**Doctrine to keep:** "Make your free stuff better than your competitor's paid stuff." "Leave your prospects better than you found them." That generosity *is* the helping-hand reveal, pre-loaded.

**Verified construction rules (his "HVCO" = High-Value Content Offer):**
- *Title it with emotionally charged words* — **secrets, mistakes, worst, fastest, proven** — solving **one narrow problem**, and **add a number.** (e.g. "7 Mistakes Most New Homeowners Make.") For you: *"5 AI Automations That Quietly Replaced a $60k Hire (And How to Steal Them)."*
- *Short is fine* — "even 2 pages or a 2-minute video." The bar is value density, not length.
- *Opt-in page structure:* powerful headline → subhead that restates the offer → punchy benefit bullets → an **image of the deliverable** (show the thing) → a simple name + email form. Nothing else competing for attention.
- *His own proof artifact:* King Kong's official 23-page "High-Converting Facebook Ads Swipe File," signed "Best, Sabri Suby, Founder, King Kong," tells readers to **"model (Read: not copy)"** — i.e. the lead magnet itself teaches the swipe method while doing lead-gen. Your tool/audit should do the same double duty.

### 9b. The hook headline (harvest tabloid structures, AI niche)

Take his 4 formulas and his tabloid word-bank ("exposed, shocked, secret, banned, the truth about, what X doesn't want you to know," + numbers) and re-skin:

- *"7 jobs your business is still paying humans to do that AI quietly did for free last Tuesday."* (formula 1 + number + curiosity)
- *"How to cut 20 hours a week of admin without hiring, firing, or learning to code — in 14 days."* (formula 4)
- *"Run your back office like a 50-person company even if it's just you and a laptop."* (formula 3)
- *"The 'illegal-feeling' AI workflow that replies to every lead in 4 seconds (your competitors are asleep)."* (curiosity + threat)
- *"We let AI run our client's intake for 30 days. The results were so good their receptionist asked for a raise."* (his "I let AI find the business" pattern)

Then run S.H.I.N.E. on each. Keep a swipe file of tabloid/Lad-Bible/TMZ headlines and transplant the *structures*.

### 9c. The Godfather Offer for AI implementation (fill-in)

1. **Rationale:** *why* the deal is so good — "We're taking on 10 new implementation clients this quarter to build case studies in [your niche], so we're doing the first build at cost." (Real, credible.)
2. **Build value:** itemize — discovery + audit ($X), custom build ($X), integration ($X), training ($X), 90-day optimization ($X), the prompt/automation library ($X). Total it; show the price as a fraction.
3. **Pricing:** value-based. Anchor against the cost of the *human* it replaces or the revenue the leak is losing them. "10% of the value" logic.
4. **Payment plans:** monthly retainer vs. build fee split.
5. **Premiums:** the swipe-file tool, a private Loom library, a "first 3 automations free," a quarterly AI-roadmap call.
6. **Power guarantee (the centerpiece):** re-skin "beat your results by 30% or we work free." For AI implementation: *"We'll save you at least [X hours / $X] a month within 90 days or we keep working free until we do."* Or *"If the automation doesn't pay for itself in 90 days, you don't pay."* Build it from their top 4–5 fears (it won't work for my business / it'll break / my team won't adopt it / I'll be locked in / it'll feel impersonal).
7. **Scarcity (real):** genuine capacity limit on builds per month, a true cohort start date, an honest intro-pricing window.

### 9d. The funnel (re-skinned 8 stages)

1. **Dream buyer (Halo):** forum-forage where your niche complains about busywork, slow lead response, can't-hire, drowning-in-admin. Steal their literal words ("I'm the bottleneck," "I'm doing $200k of work for free at night").
2. **Bait:** the free tool/build/audit (§9a).
3. **Traffic:** ads whose only job is the click; tabloid headlines (§9b).
4. **Capture:** opt-in for the tool. Minimal fields.
5. **Nurture/indoctrinate:** short value emails + content; agitate the cost of staying manual.
6. **Magic Lantern (the helping-hand bridge):** a VSL/email/video that *teaches* — names the problem, walks through the existing "solutions" (hiring, generic SaaS, DIY ChatGPT) and why each falls short, then pivots. **This is where the reveal copy lives** (§9e).
7. **Convert:** free strategy/implementation call → the Godfather Offer.
8. **Multiply:** retainers, referrals, upsell to broader implementation, productize a low-ticket tool tier (your Kong).

### 9e. The "caught you / helping hand" bridge copy (drop-in template)

> "Look — I'll be honest with you. I hooked you in with [the free AI tool / that headline]. That was on purpose, because it's the fastest way to show you what's actually possible now instead of just telling you.
>
> But here's who we really are. We're not here to sell you a gadget and disappear. We're an AI implementation team, and our whole thing is unglamorous: we sit down, find the 3–4 places where you're bleeding hours or money, and we *build the thing that fixes it* — then we make sure your team actually uses it.
>
> The free tool you just got? That's a fraction of what we do for clients every week. We gave it away because, frankly, the best way to earn the right to help you with the big stuff is to make your life measurably better today, before you've paid us a cent.
>
> So if it helped — good. If you want to see how much further this goes, the next step is a free 30-minute build session where we map the highest-leverage automation in *your* business, live. No pitch theater. Worst case, you walk away with a plan you can build yourself."

That paragraph runs the whole Sabri machine: value-first honesty, the reveal, the generosity frame, "leave them better than you found them," a valued-but-free next step, and self-selection — in the operator-with-an-edge voice, dialed to whatever profanity level your brand allows.

### 9f. Voice calibration for an AI agency

Keep the **specificity, proof-stacking, contrarian framing, benefit-over-feature, and the honesty caveat.** Dial the profanity to your brand's tolerance (Sabri's nuclear cursing is a *choice*, not a requirement — the engine works clean). Lead with the *outcome they lie awake wanting* (their time back, leads answered instantly, scaling without headcount), never with "we use GPT/agents/RAG." Features tell; benefits sell.

### 9g. Design starting point

One loud accent on white, condensed bold headlines + Inter body, relentlessly repeated action-command CTA, proof counters and named result quotes, video near the top, a guarantee band you can defend. (Don't copy King Kong's lime; pick your own single accent so you read as *inspired-by*, not *clone-of*.)

## 10. Honesty / ethics guardrails (important for the "helping hand" to be true)

The whole positioning collapses if the "we just want to help" is a costume. To keep it real:
- **Scarcity must be real** (his own rule). Don't fake stock counters or cohort limits.
- **Guarantees must be honest and honored** — and structured so you can actually hit them.
- **Proof must be defensible.** Use *your* real numbers; "marketers lie, numbers don't" cuts both ways. Don't borrow the $7.8B aura without your own substance.
- **Deliver more free value than feels comfortable.** That's what makes the reveal land instead of feeling like a bait-and-switch.
