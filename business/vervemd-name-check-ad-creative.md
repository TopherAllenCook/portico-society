# VerveMD AI Name Check: Ad Creative

> Ready-to-run ads built from the 10 funnel headlines, all driving to the new bait at **vervemd.com/name-check** (which hands off to the free audit). Voice: operator with an edge, restrained. Rules honored throughout: no em or en dashes, no exclamation points, no emojis, no invented numbers (only the BrightLocal 60% figure, attributed, and "around three names" as an estimate), plain English, second person, one destination.
>
> `[trade]` and `[city]` are placeholders. Run one ad set per trade (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, roofing) and localize the city. Trade nouns: plumber, HVAC company, electrician, roofer.

> Testing note: Ads 1 and 4 lead with the owner's blind spot (broadest appeal). Ad 2 is the sharpest (competitor by name) and usually the highest click rate, but qualify hard. Ad 3 anchors on the one credible stat for skeptics. Rotate headlines across ads to find the winner, then scale the single best.

## D. Guardrails for whoever runs these
- One destination only: vervemd.com/name-check. The Name Check hands to the free audit; do not also push the booking call in the ad. Keep one ask.
- The only number you may state is the BrightLocal 60% figure, with the attribution. "Around three names" must stay framed as an estimate. Invent nothing else (no client counts, no results, no testimonials).
- No em dashes, no exclamation points, no emojis. Keep the restrained register; this audience distrusts hype.
- Localize [trade] and [city] per ad set. Do not run a generic "home services" ad where a trade-specific one will speak louder.
