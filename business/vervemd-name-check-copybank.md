## Honesty guardrails (non-negotiable)
The skill's own rule and VerveMD's brand rule agree: the promise must match delivery, and we never invent numbers or fabricate client results.
- The only external stat allowed is **60% of home-service searches start with an AI tool (BrightLocal, 2025)**, used once and attributed.
- "About three names" is always framed as **our estimate**.
- **No fabricated testimonials or job counts.** Confession/testimonial headlines (e.g. "I now book 30+ jobs a month") only run once a real, named client actually says it. Everything below is written to hit hard without faking a result.
- No em dashes, no exclamation points, no emojis baked into the image (emoji belong in the caption, added at post time).

## 2. Headline bank, by hook type (A/B-ready)
`[trade]` = plumber / HVAC company / electrician / roofer. Each tagged hook → power word → psychological trigger.

**Curiosity gap** (brain needs closure)
- Here's what AI tells your next customer about you. You've never seen it. → *Truth / open loop*
- There's a short list AI reads to every homeowner. Your name may not be on it. → *curiosity gap*

**Data shock** (60%, attributed)
- 60% of home-service searches now start with AI, not Google. Are you in the answer? → *surprise / pattern interrupt*

**Fear / loss aversion**
- If AI doesn't name you, you're invisible to customers who never call. → *Invisible / loss aversion*
- Warning: the jobs you're losing don't show up as missed calls. They never reach you. → *Warning / fear*

**Question**
- When a homeowner asks AI for the best [trade] in your city, does your name come up? → *recognition*
- Do you know what AI says about your business? → *curiosity*

**Controversy / contrarian**
- Google is no longer where homeowners find you. Most [trade]s haven't noticed. → *cognitive dissonance*
- Stop renting shared leads. Start owning the answer AI gives. → *Stop / challenges norm*

**Insider secret**
- The free 60-second check that shows if AI recommends you (most owners skip it). → *Free / Secret / FOMO*

**Direct address / problem call**
- Great at the work, invisible online? Here's the one-minute fix to check. → *personal relevance*

**Confession / testimonial (HOLD until a real client says it)**
- "I was invisible when people asked AI. One check changed that." → *vulnerability / relatability* (needs real attribution before running)

## 6. Psychological trigger map + A/B plan
| Creative angle | Primary trigger | When it wins |
|---|---|---|
| Curiosity ("you've never seen it") | Curiosity gap | Cold audiences, broad reach |
| Fear ("invisible to customers who never call") | Loss aversion | Owners already worried about slow months |
| Data ("60% start with AI") | Authority / social proof | Skeptics who distrust hype |
| Question ("does your name come up?") | Recognition | Mid-funnel, retargeting |
| Contrarian ("Google isn't where they find you") | Cognitive dissonance | Pattern interrupt, scroll-stop |

**First test:** same photo + NEWS style, swap only the headline across Curiosity vs Fear vs Data. Curiosity and fear usually win cold; the 60% stat wins with skeptics. Kill the loser after ~3 days and run the winner against the Question and Contrarian angles.
