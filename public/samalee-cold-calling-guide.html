VerveMD Cold Calling Guide

Roger framework — Hook → Diagnose → Handoff. Not a script. A diagnostic approach.

THE GOLDEN RULEYou are a digital doctor. You don't prescribe until you've diagnosed. The more questions you ask, the more they trust you. They arrive at the solution themselves — you never tell them what they need.

1. THE HOOK

No pleasantries. No "how's business going." No introducing yourself or VerveMD. You have three seconds.

"Hey, are you all fully booked this month or are you able to take on more patients."

For med spas and aesthetic practices:

"Hey, you all completely booked this month or could your team handle a few more appointments."

Downward inflection on the last word. Not upward. It signals confidence. It stops them. About half will say some version of "we'd love more" or "it's been slow." You're in.

NO GATEKEEPER SCRIPTYou do not introduce yourself, explain VerveMD, or ask for the marketing person. The hook IS the opener — it works on receptionists and practice owners alike. If a receptionist answers, ask the hook. If they're not the decision maker, they'll tell you who to talk to. The hook still works on them because they know whether the practice is booked or not.

2. DIAGNOSE

Ask questions. Let them tell you the pain. Never pitch during diagnosis.

IDEAL FLOW

You: "Hey, are you all fully booked this month or are you able to take on more patients."

Them: "We would love more work actually. It's been kind of slow."

You: "Oh really. I'm sorry to hear that." [pause] "What do you think's caused the slow season."

Them: "I don't know. Word of mouth just slowed down."

You: "Yeah, that makes sense. Are you doing anything online right now."

Them: "No. We know we need to be. Our competitors are. I just don't know how to do any of that."

You: "If you were to start, what would you want that to look like. Or is it too early to say."

Them: "I just want people finding us. Instead of our competitors showing up first."

At this point they've said the pain out loud. You didn't sell anything. You diagnosed.

3. HANDOFF

Only after they've said the pain out loud. Chris is the closer. You are the opener.

"Okay. So my partner Chris — he works with practices on exactly this. Getting found by the patients who are already searching. He starts with a free audit, no obligation — just shows where you stand online. Would it make sense for you two to talk."

If yes: "Perfect. What's the best number for him to reach you. And what email should he send the audit to."

If hesitant: "Totally fair. It's just a conversation — if nothing else, you'll know exactly where you stand. No obligation. Worth fifteen minutes."

NEVER send a brochure. "I'd rather just connect you with Chris. He does a free audit specific to your practice — it'll be more useful than a brochure."

5. VOICEMAIL

Leave sparingly. Most won't call back — that's normal. Try twice, leave one voicemail, move on.

"Hey, this is Samalee with VerveMD, calling for [practice name]. Chris works with practices on getting found by patients who are searching for them online. He does a free audit, no obligation. Give me a call back if that's worth a look — [number]. Thanks."

Under 20 seconds.

6. CRITICAL TECHNIQUES

  • Downward inflection: End every sentence with tone moving down, not up. Upward inflection signals uncertainty and triggers resistance.
  • Questions and stories: No feature lists. No pitches. Ask questions. Tell stories. When someone hears their own pain out loud, they arrive at wanting the solution themselves.
  • Genuinely care: Pause after "oh really." Let their answer land. Don't rush to the next line.
  • Graceful exit: If they say no twice, thank them and move on. "Got it. Thanks for your time." Leaves the door open.
  • THE ONLY METRIC: Handoffs booked per day. Two to three is great. Twenty to thirty dials is a realistic rhythm.

7. CALL LOGGING

After every call, log these five things:

  1. Practice name and phone number
  2. Who you spoke to (name, role)
  3. Outcome: Handoff booked / Call back later / Not interested / No answer / Left voicemail
  4. If handoff — website URL, primary city, best email, best phone
  5. What they said the problem was — Chris leads with this on his call. This is the most important thing you write down.

WHAT HAPPENS WHEN YOU BOOK A HANDOFF

Tell them this so they show up curious, not defensive:

"Chris runs a quick audit on your practice before the call — sees where you're showing up online and where you're not. On the call he walks you through what he found and what it would take to fix it. No obligation. Just a conversation."
