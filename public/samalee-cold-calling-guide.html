VerveMD Cold Calling Guide

Version 2. Hook → Story → Offer. A skeleton, not a recital. Memorize the beats, then say it in your own cadence.

THE GOLDEN RULEYou are a digital doctor. You don't prescribe until you've diagnosed. The more questions you ask, the more they trust you. They arrive at the solution themselves. You never tell them what they need.

0. HOW TO USE THIS

This is a skeleton, not a script you read. Memorize the beats and the branch logic, then say it your way. Read it out loud until the pauses feel natural and it sounds like you, not like a page. Tone beats words every time. If you sound like you are reading, you lose them in the first five seconds.

1. THE GOAL

Two ways to win on every good call. You never leave a good call empty-handed.

  1. Primary: book a 15-minute discovery call on Chris's calendar with the decision-maker.
  2. Backup that is almost as good: if you cannot book live, capture the six audit fields so the free audit fires and the automated email sequence books them for you over the next few weeks.

Ideally you get both: book the call, and still capture the audit so it lands in their inbox before you talk.

2. BEFORE YOU DIAL

  • Pull the clinic's team or about page. Note the owner or doctor and whoever handles marketing or patient growth, by name. Ask for that person by name, not "whoever handles marketing."
  • Have the booking link ready to text the second they say yes: cal.com/vervemd/audit-review.
  • Best dial windows: mid-morning around 10am, and early afternoon around 1:30 to 3pm. Avoid open, lunch, and close, when the front desk is slammed.

3. THE PHONE TREE

You will often hit automation before a human. Get to a live person fast:

  1. Listen for a "new patient" or "scheduling" or "appointments" option and take it. That is the line they actually staff, and that person can transfer you or hand you the name you need.
  2. No new-patient option? Press 0 for the operator or front desk. Sometimes more than once.
  3. Dial-by-name directory and you have the name from the team page? Use it.
  4. If it dumps you into a department voicemail, hang up. Do not leave a message there. Mark it and try again in a different window. See the voicemail policy.

4. THE HOOK

React warmly to whatever they said when they picked up. Quick, two seconds. Not "how are you today." Just a real human beat, a first-name intro, then the question.

"Hey, that's awesome. I'm Sam." [half a beat] "I just wanna know, are you guys fully booked right now, or could you handle a few more patients."

Downward inflection on "patients." Then shut up. Don't fill the silence. About half will say some version of "we'd love more." You're in.

5. DISARM

Only after they answer. This line is about not running the business. It is never about being or not being a clinician.

"Got it. So real quick, I'm not a clinic owner, I've never run one. But I'm pretty good at getting the right patients to find clinics like yours and start calling. I'd love to send people your way if it's a fit."

Swap "clinic owner" for "med spa owner" or "practice owner" depending on what you are dialing.

6. DIAGNOSE

Ask questions. Let them name the pain. Never pitch during diagnosis. One question at a time, wait for each answer.

  1. "When new patients walk in the door, where do they usually say they found you. Word of mouth, Google, ads, a mix."
  2. "And of those, which channel is actually bringing you the patients you want more of."
  3. "Is there a type of patient or treatment you wish you saw more of, but you just can't seem to get them in the door."
  4. "(only if they're still talking) When you've tried marketing in the past, what's been the most frustrating part."

7. THE MIRROR

They convince themselves. Only after this do you offer.

"Okay, so what I'm hearing is, you're getting [X] but not enough [Y], and the last time you tried [Z] it didn't really do much. Sound about right."

8. THE OFFER (double-point close)

Lead with the live booking. If they won't commit to a time, fall back to the audit. Try for both.

"Got it. Here's what I'd do. I work alongside Chris, he's the strategist who's been getting clinics in this exact spot more of the right patients. He's doing free 15-minute calls this week, no pitch, just walks through whether it'd be a fit. Worth grabbing a spot Thursday or Friday."

If yes, book live: "Awesome. I'm texting you the link right now. Grab Thursday 2pm or Friday 10am, whichever works."

If they won't pick a time, go to the audit: "All good. Let me just run you a free audit that shows where your clinic shows up when patients search the new way, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google. No cost. What's the best email and your website."

Either way, capture the six fields below. Hand them to Chris or drop them into the New Audit tool.

9. IF THEY ASK "WHAT DO YOU GUYS DO?"

Do not describe a service. Answer with the question. This is your strongest line. Use it any time, gatekeeper or decision-maker.

"Honestly, easiest way to show you: do you know if your clinic shows up when someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Claude for the best [med spa / longevity clinic] in [city]." [pause] "Most clinics don't, and that's where a real chunk of patients are starting to look now. I run a free check that shows you exactly where you land. Want me to run yours."

11. END-OF-CALL CAPTURE (six fields)

Before you hang up on any interested call, get these six. The first five are required for the audit to run. The sixth makes the report sharper.

  1. Clinic name
  2. Their name (first and last) and title
  3. Best email
  4. Website URL
  5. City (primary location)
  6. What kind of clinic: longevity, concierge, aesthetic, or a mix

Nice to have: their phone, and the one type of patient or treatment they wish they saw more of. That last one goes straight into the audit as their stated challenge.

12. WHAT HAPPENS AFTER YOU CAPTURE

Hand the six fields to Chris, or drop them straight into the New Audit tool. From there the system runs itself:

  • Fires the audit on their website.
  • Emails them an acknowledgement right away.
  • Delivers the full audit report when it's ready.
  • Runs a five-touch email sequence over about 45 days, each with a booking link.

This is the double-point: they can book from the link you texted live, or from any email in the sequence. The moment they book either way, the sequence automatically stops, so they never get double-messaged. You don't have to track any of it.

13. VOICEMAIL

  • Attempts 1 and 2: no voicemail. Voicemails from an unknown number rarely get returned, and they tip your hand before you ever get to talk.
  • Attempt 3 and beyond: optional 10-second curiosity voicemail, then immediately send the free-audit email so there's a concrete reason to call back.
  • Never pitch on a voicemail. Never leave the offer. The only job of the voicemail is to make a callback feel worth it.
"Hey [Name], it's Sam. Quick one about how your clinic shows up when patients search the new way, ChatGPT and Google. Not a sales thing, I'll shoot you a short email too. Talk soon."

Under 10 seconds.

14. CRITICAL TECHNIQUES

  • Downward inflection: end every sentence with tone moving down, not up. Upward inflection signals uncertainty and triggers resistance.
  • Pauses are weapons: after the opener question, after the disarm, after each diagnostic question. Silence makes them talk.
  • Never prescribe before they name the pain: jump to "I help with AI search" before they tell you what's broken and you're the doctor handing out medicine before taking a history. They won't trust it.
  • The opener never names the company: "I'm Sam with VerveMD" flags it as a sales call in three seconds. First name only.
  • "I," not "we": "we" sounds like a company they have to evaluate.
  • Graceful exit: if they say no twice, thank them and move on. "Got it, thanks for your time." Leaves the door open.

15. CALL LOGGING

After every call, log these. Three weeks of this tells you which opener works, which clinic size converts, and where people drop off.

  1. Clinic name and phone number
  2. Did you hit automation, and how did you get past it
  3. Who you spoke to (name, role)
  4. Booked live? Yes or no. Audit captured? Yes or no.
  5. What they said the problem was. Chris leads with this on his call. The most important thing you write down.
