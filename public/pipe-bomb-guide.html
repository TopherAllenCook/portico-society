Verve MD Pipe Bomb Guide

Personalized Loom outreach. The sniper rifle that runs alongside cold email and calls. A skeleton, not a recital. Say it like a person who actually looked at their business, because you did.

THE RULE80 percent value, 20 percent pitch. The video proves you can help by actually helping. Its only job is to open a conversation, not close one. If you sound like you are selling, you have already lost them.

0. WHAT THIS IS

A two to three minute screen-and-face Loom recorded for one specific prospect. You bring their own website or Google profile up on screen, run a live AI search, show them a real gap, and offer the audit. It is the high-trust closer that the automated audit on its own cannot be. Use it where the human effort converts, not at random.

1. WHEN TO RECORD ONE

  1. Best trigger: the moment Instantly shows a prospect opened the audit. Their attention is hot. Record while it is.
  2. Also good: any top-fit lead in the workspace (high ICP score) worth ten minutes of your time.
  3. Cadence: aim for five to ten per day. Not everyone replies. That is fine.

2. THE BEATS

1. Identity trigger 0:00 to 0:10

Open on their thing already on screen. They see their own business. That stops the scroll.

Hey [Name], it's Chris from Verve. That's your Google profile up on my screen. Give me ninety seconds, this is specific to [Company], then you can get back to your day.

2. The live proof 0:10 to 1:00

Do the thing in real time. Do not tell, show.

I just asked ChatGPT for the best [trade] in [city]. Watch what comes back. [show the three names] [Company] is not in there. That is not a knock on your work. The search moved, and a real chunk of homeowners start right here now.

3. The diagnosis 1:00 to 2:00

Two or three specific gaps. Plain English, no acronyms.

Here is why. Your Google profile is missing your service area. Your most recent reviews are from a while back and nobody has replied to them. And when I fill out your contact form, here is where it goes. None of this is complicated, and honestly that is the point. The shops getting named are just doing the basics on purpose.

4. The helping hand 2:00 to 2:30

The reveal, the generosity, the two-path close.

Now, I already ran a full audit on [Company]. I am happy to just send it over. It is specific to you, way more useful than anything I could pitch. If it helps, fix this yourself, genuinely. Or if you want, we spend fifteen minutes and I walk you through it. Your call, no obligation either way.

5. The low-friction ask

Easiest thing: just reply to my email and I will send the audit over. Talk soon.

3. FILLED EXAMPLE (HVAC, one breath)

Hey Marcus, it's Chris from Verve. That's Day and Night Heating up on my screen. Ninety seconds, this is just about your shop. I asked ChatGPT who the best HVAC company in Mesa is, here is the three it named. You are not one of them, and right before the summer rush that is the search filling other people's calendars. A couple reasons why: your profile is missing your service area, your last review reply was a year ago, and your site never actually answers "how much is a new AC." I built you a full audit on it already. Want it? Reply and it is yours. Or grab fifteen minutes and I will walk you through fixing it. Either way.

4. VOICE RULES (same as everything Verve)

  • No dashes, no exclamation points, no emojis, no made-up numbers.
  • Plain English. You are the good tech who looked at the system before quoting, not the pushy salesman.
  • Lead with their booked jobs and owned pipeline, never with "we use AI."
  • Tone beats words. Record it twice. Send the one that sounds like you.
