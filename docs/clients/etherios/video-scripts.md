# Etherios — Pre-Consult Video Scripts

Four 30-second videos to embed in the post-booking sequence. Each addresses a specific drop-off cause and slots into a specific touchpoint.

**Production notes for all four:**
- Shot in the actual clinic or the clinician's actual office. Not a studio. Not a green screen.
- Clinician wears what they'd wear to a consult. No white coat unless they actually wear one.
- Direct-to-camera, eye-level, single take preferred. No B-roll cuts mid-sentence.
- Caption all videos. ~40% of patients watch on mute, and the accessibility matters for this population.
- Keep the URL where the video lives stable. Embed by link in email, not as attachment.

---

## Video 1 — "What this call actually is"

**Goal:** Kill the "is this a sales pitch?" objection before it forms.
**Slot into sequence:** Embedded in the immediate confirmation email (touch #1).
**On camera:** The clinician the patient is booked with.
**Length:** ~30 seconds, 74 words.

---

> Hi, I'm [Clinician Name]. If you booked a consult with us, you'll see me on the call.
>
> I want to be clear about what that call is. It's a medical conversation. I'll ask about your history, what you've tried, and what you're hoping for. I'll tell you honestly whether ketamine or Spravato is a fit, or whether something else would serve you better.
>
> You won't be asked to commit to anything. See you soon.

**Why this works:**
- Names the fear ("is this a sales call?") and dismantles it in plain language.
- "I'll tell you honestly whether... something else would serve you better" is the line that earns trust. It signals the clinician will turn business away if it's not right. Patients have never heard that from a competing clinic.
- Closing with "See you soon" presumes the appointment rather than begging for it.

## Video 2 — "Meet your clinician + Spravato primer"

**Goal:** Put a face to the consult and give the patient something to read so they feel prepared rather than ambushed.
**Slot into sequence:** Embedded in the personal clinician note (touch #2) or in a standalone email at T+24 hours after booking.
**On camera:** The clinician the patient is booked with, in the consult room they'll actually use.
**Length:** ~30 seconds, 76 words.

---

> Hi, I'm [Clinician Name]. I'll be the face you see on your consultation call.
>
> On the call, we'll go over anything you want to talk through — your history, what you're hoping for, questions you've been holding on to. Nothing is off the table.
>
> In the meantime, I've sent over a short guide to Spravato and how we work with it here at Etherios. Take a look when you have a minute. We'll pick it up from there.

**Why this works:**
- "The face you see on your consultation call" lands harder than "I'll be your clinician." It tells the patient exactly what to expect visually, which lowers anticipatory anxiety.
- "Nothing is off the table" gives explicit permission to bring up cost, fear, family pushback, prior bad experiences — the things patients usually self-censor.
- Pairing the video with a written Spravato primer means the patient has homework that doesn't feel like homework. They arrive informed, which makes the consult more productive and the no-show less likely.

**Companion asset required:** A 1-page Spravato primer (PDF or web page). Should cover: what it is, what a session looks like, the 2-hour observation period, the REMS requirement, insurance reality, and what makes Etherios's approach specific. Do not skip this — the video promises it.

## Video 3 — "About the cost"

**Goal:** Remove cost ambiguity before the patient talks to a partner or parent who asks "how much?"
**Slot into sequence:** Embedded in the T-72 hours email (touch #3).
**On camera:** Either the clinician or the practice's intake coordinator. Whoever actually handles billing conversations.
**Length:** ~30 seconds, 76 words.

---

> Quick note about cost, because I know it's on your mind.
>
> If you're exploring Spravato, we're running your insurance this week and you'll have a real number before we meet. If you're exploring ketamine-assisted therapy, it's out-of-pocket, and the price per session is on our site. No bundles you have to commit to. No surprises at the visit.
>
> If the number doesn't work, tell us on the call. We'll be straight with you.

**Why this works:**
- Most ketamine clinics dodge pricing until the patient is in the chair. Saying the quiet part out loud — that it's on their mind — disarms it.
- "No bundles you have to commit to" addresses the specific anxiety patients have after reading reviews of high-pressure clinics that push 6-session packages.
- "Tell us on the call. We'll be straight with you" makes it safe to raise cost objections in person, which keeps patients from ghosting because they assumed they couldn't afford it.

---

## Video 4 — "If today doesn't feel like the day"

**Goal:** Convert no-shows into reschedules. This is the highest-leverage video in the set.
**Slot into sequence:** Linked from the T-3 hour day-of SMS (touch #5) and from the no-show recovery SMS (touch #6).
**On camera:** Clinician. Warmth matters more here than polish.
**Length:** ~30 seconds, 72 words.

---

> If you're watching this, you probably booked a consult and you're not sure you want to show up. That's not unusual. The thing we're treating is often the same thing making it hard to walk in the door.
>
> You have two options. Show up as you are, even if that's tired or uncertain. Or move it to a day that fits better. Both are fine. Ghosting it is the only one that doesn't help you.

**Why this works:**
- Names the patient's exact internal experience. "The thing we're treating is often the same thing making it hard to walk in the door" is the line that lands. It tells the patient their ambivalence is information, not a failure.
- Offers two real options and explicitly closes the third (ghosting). That framing is what flips the no-show into a reschedule click.
- Do not soften this script. The directness is the medicine.

---

## Implementation order

If production bandwidth is limited, shoot in this order:

1. **Video 4 first.** Highest impact on show rate. Goes in the SMS that recovers the most patients.
2. **Video 1 second.** Sets the tone for the entire funnel and reduces the comparison-shopping problem.
3. **Video 2 third.** Pairs with the Spravato primer and gives patients something to do while they wait.
4. **Video 3 last.** Important, but the written cost language in the T-72 email carries most of the load even without video.

One shoot day with the clinician on camera should yield all four plus alternates.

---

## What we're explicitly not making

- A founder origin story
- A "meet the team" montage
- Patient testimonials (ethical and HIPAA risk in this category, and they read as manipulative to a population that's already wary of being sold to)
- Anything with stock B-roll of brain scans, lotus flowers, or sunsets

The clinician, talking directly to the patient, in a real room. That's the brief.
