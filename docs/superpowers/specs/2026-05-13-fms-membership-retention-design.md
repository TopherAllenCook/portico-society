# FMS Membership and Retention Strategy — Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-05-13
**Client:** Financial Managers Society (fmsinc.org)
**Contacts:** Alana Vartanian, President & CEO / Jennifer Lindberg, Director of Marketing & Communications
**Deadline:** June board meeting (exact date unknown — assume 3-6 weeks)
**Engagement type:** Freelance — full audit + strategy + campaign execution

---

## Context

FMS is a professional association serving financial managers at community banks, savings institutions, and credit unions. They offer education, certifications, events (webinars, seminars, annual conference), and peer networking.

Chris has admin access to:
- **Informz** — their email marketing platform (via Higher Logic)
- **LinkedIn** — FMS company page content admin

FMS already has a point-based member engagement scoring system built from 2025 activity (webinars, seminars, annual conference attendance). Tier thresholds: A = 40+ points, B = ~27 points, descending from there. This infrastructure exists but campaigns are not fully attached to it.

The goal Alana stated: "get emails and campaigns updated and active before the June board meeting."

---

## Approach: Parallel Tracks

Two tracks run simultaneously rather than sequentially.

**Track 1 — Quick Wins:** Build and launch campaigns in the first 1-2 weeks. Campaigns are actively running by the time the board meeting happens.

**Track 2 — Audit + Strategy:** Audit Informz and LinkedIn in parallel, build the strategy from findings, and produce the board deliverable.

The board meeting then receives three things: active campaigns already running, audit findings, and a 90-day strategy. The framing is "here's what we fixed, here's what we learned, here's what comes next."

## Section 1: Audit Framework

### Informz Audit
Pull the last 12 months of sends and measure:
- List size and growth/decay trend
- Open rates (association benchmark: 30-35%)
- Click-through rates
- Unsubscribe rate and timing patterns
- Campaign cadence and frequency
- Segment usage (member vs. non-member, tier-based, event-based)
- Content audit — stale subject lines, outdated event references, broken links, campaigns unchanged for 2+ years

Diagnostic questions:
- Is the list growing or decaying?
- Are open rates trending down (content fatigue)?
- Are there member lifecycle moments with no email?

### LinkedIn Audit
- Follower count and 90-day growth trend
- Engagement rate per post (likes, comments, shares)
- Content mix breakdown (educational vs. promotional vs. event)
- Posting frequency and consistency
- What content has performed best

Diagnostic questions:
- Is there a consistent posting cadence?
- What content type drives the most engagement?
- Does the current voice reflect FMS's value proposition?

### Engagement Tier Analysis
Using FMS's existing point-based scoring system:
- Map the full tier distribution: what % of members are A (40+), B (~27), C, and disengaged
- Identify where retention risk is concentrated
- Identify the intervention window (members who don't move up a tier within their first 90 days rarely do)

## Section 2: Quick Wins (Week 1-2)

Three campaigns built and sent before any other deliverable.

### Campaign 1 — Re-engagement (Low-Engagement Current Members)
**Target:** C-tier and disengaged members who are still active in the system -- current members who have gone quiet, not former members who already cancelled

3-email sequence:
1. Surface the value they're missing — upcoming events, peer network, education credits. Specific, not generic.
2. A low-friction re-entry point — free webinar, resource download, something with no commitment required.
3. A plain-text personal note from Alana. No design, no template. The most effective re-engagement email is the one that feels human.

Subject line principle: urgency and specificity over sentiment ("we miss you" language is low-performing).

**Rationale:** Members who already paid once are far easier to re-activate than acquiring new ones. Highest ROI send available.

### Campaign 2 — New Member Welcome Sequence
**Target:** New members within their first 30 days

3-email sequence:
1. Day 1 — Welcome with a clear "here's what to do first." One action, not a list of ten.
2. Day 7 — Introduction to the engagement scoring system framed as a game: here's your current score, here's how to move up, here's what's coming.
3. Day 30 — Check-in highlighting upcoming events and peer community touchpoints.

**Rationale:** First-year churn is the most common failure point in associations. A strong welcome sequence is the single highest-leverage retention intervention.

### Campaign 3 — LinkedIn Activation
Post 3 pieces of content in the first two weeks:
1. Thought leadership piece aimed at senior members (CFOs, controllers, VPs at community banks)
2. Event or education spotlight — something upcoming that demonstrates active programming
3. Member value post — why FMS membership matters, written in plain language for non-members who encounter it

**Rationale:** Establishes visible momentum on LinkedIn before the board meeting. Also begins top-of-funnel for acquisition.

## Section 3: Membership Growth and Retention Strategy

### Retention

**Tier-to-churn mapping**
The audit will surface where members are dropping. Members who don't move up a tier within their first 90 days almost never do. That window is the intervention point for the welcome sequence -- catch them early or lose them at renewal.

**Trigger-based communication system**
Attach campaigns to the engagement scoring system FMS already built:
- Members who haven't attended an event in 60 days → targeted email surfacing upcoming event
- Members approaching renewal with low point scores → personal outreach from Alana or Jennifer
- Members who attended the annual conference but have no other activity → follow-up campaign converting that one touchpoint into ongoing engagement

**Renewal sequence**
Three sends before expiration:
- 90 days out: frame renewal around what's coming (upcoming events, new content, peer value)
- 30 days out: reminder with specifics — what they've attended, what's ahead
- Final week: direct, brief, personal

No "your membership expires soon" generic language. Every renewal email frames forward value.

### Acquisition

**LinkedIn as top-of-funnel**
Financial professionals at community banks and credit unions are on LinkedIn. The content cadence from the quick wins phase sustains as a consistent drumbeat. Educational content that demonstrates FMS value to non-members is a soft pitch in every post.

**Referral activation via email**
A-tier members (40+ points) are the best acquisition channel FMS has. A specific referral ask outperforms a vague invitation. Not "invite a colleague" — "who on your team would benefit from the annual conference?" Give the ask a specific context and a low-friction submission method.

**Event attendee pipeline**
People who attended FMS events in 2025 but are not current members are almost certainly already in Informz. They have already raised their hand. A dedicated email campaign targeting this segment -- "you've attended, here's what you're missing as a member" -- is the warmest cold acquisition audience available. Pull this segment from Informz during the audit and build a 2-email sequence: email 1 surfaces specific member benefits they didn't get access to, email 2 is a direct membership offer with a clear CTA.

**Lead magnet via LinkedIn**
LinkedIn content builds awareness but doesn't capture anyone. A free resource -- salary benchmark, regulatory update summary, industry trends report -- gives non-members a reason to hand over their email. Post the offer on LinkedIn, collect emails via a simple form, and route them into Informz for the non-member nurture sequence. The lead magnet topic should come from whatever content performs best in the LinkedIn audit.

**Non-member nurture sequence**
If Informz contains non-member contacts (event registrants who didn't join, lapsed former members, lead magnet downloads), build a 3-email nurture sequence: email 1 frames the problem FMS solves for their career, email 2 surfaces a specific high-value benefit (education credits, certification, peer network), email 3 is a direct membership offer. This segment likely already exists -- the audit will confirm its size.

**Corporate/group membership pitch**
Community banks and credit unions often have multiple finance team members who could benefit from FMS. A single email to the CFO or HR director at a target institution -- pitching a group membership covering their full finance team -- converts one outreach into 3-5 members. Build a short, specific email targeting finance leadership at institutions in FMS's existing member geography. One send, high leverage.

## Section 4: Board Deliverable

### Format
A single clean document. Not a slide deck. One-page executive summary at the top, followed by four sections.

### Structure
1. **What we found** — Audit findings. Lead with 1-2 sharp numbers the board hasn't seen before. Open rate trend, engagement tier distribution, LinkedIn growth. Make the problem concrete with data.
2. **What we launched** — Quick win campaigns already running. Proves execution, not just planning.
3. **What we recommend** — 90-day strategy with clear milestones.
4. **How we measure it** — KPI dashboard going forward.

### Narrative Arc
The board needs to feel two things: FMS has a real problem worth taking seriously, and it's already being addressed. Audit findings create urgency. Active campaigns create confidence. Strategy gives them something to vote on or fund.

### 90-Day Roadmap
- **Month 1:** Audit complete, re-engagement and welcome campaigns live, LinkedIn cadence established
- **Month 2:** Re-engagement results in, trigger-based retention system mapped, renewal sequence drafted
- **Month 3:** Renewal sequence live, referral campaign launched, full KPI baseline set

### Ongoing KPIs
- Email: open rate, click rate, unsubscribe rate
- LinkedIn: follower growth, engagement rate per post
- Membership: engagement tier distribution (% A, B, C, disengaged)
- Renewal rate
