## STEP 1 — Brainstorm Your Idea With Claude

The hardest part of starting is knowing what to sell. That's where Claude becomes your new creative partner. Give it your background and it'll generate more ideas than you can handle.

Pick one idea that:

- Feels fun (or at least interesting) to create
- Solves a real, specific problem for your audience
- Can be delivered as a PDF, template, or guide

**Copy & paste this into Claude right now:**

> Act as an expert digital product strategist with 10+ years of experience. I want to create a digital product that generates $1,000/day in revenue.
>
> My background: [describe your skills, experience, interests]
> My target audience: [describe who you want to serve]
> My launch budget: [your budget]
> My timeline: [weeks/months]
>
> Do the following:
> 1. Identify the 3 most viable product ideas, ranked by speed-to-profit and market demand
> 2. Give me the full blueprint for the #1 product
> 3. Build a $1,000/day revenue math model
> 4. Give me a 30-day launch plan with specific daily actions
> 5. Tell me the #1 mistake people make and how I avoid it
> 6. Recommend the exact tools and platforms to build and sell

**Mini Action:** Choose ONE idea right now and write it at the top of your notes. That's your $1K product seed.

## STEP 2 — Bring It to Life in Canva

Now that you have your idea, let's make it real. Head to Canva.com — it's free and requires zero design experience.

What you can create in Canva:

- Digital planners & guided journals
- Printable checklists & habit trackers
- Ebooks, mini-guides & how-to PDFs
- Social media templates & content calendars
- Business planners & budget worksheets

**Use Claude to fill your content — for example:**

> "Write 10 positive affirmations for new moms."
> "Write a one-page intro for a goal-setting planner."
> "List 20 productivity tips for busy entrepreneurs."
> "Write 5 chapters for a beginner guide on [your topic]."

Paste the text into your Canva template, customize the colours and fonts, and you have your product.

**Pro Tip:** Use Canva's "Elements" tab for icons and borders. Stick with 2 colours and 1–2 fonts. Simplicity sells.

---

## STEP 3 — Package & Price It

Before you sell your product, make it feel premium. The packaging is what turns a good product into an irresistible one.

**Export from Canva.** Export as PDF (Print quality) for guides, workbooks, and planners. Export as PNG for social media templates.

**Create a product thumbnail.** In Canva, search "ebook mockup" or "digital product mockup". Place your cover on a tablet or desk scene — this is what buyers see first.

**Set your price.** For a solid PDF guide: $27 is the sweet spot. Low enough to be an impulse buy, high enough to signal real value. 38 sales at $27 = $1,000. You can always raise it later.

**Mini Action:** Decide your price right now. Write it down. Progress beats perfection every time.
