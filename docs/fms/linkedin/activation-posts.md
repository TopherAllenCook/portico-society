# LinkedIn Activation Posts

**Status:** Draft, ready for review. Revised against audit findings in `docs/fms/audits/audit-linkedin-public.md`.

**Audit findings shaping this content:**
- FMS posts ~10-12x per week with ~90% promotional content and median 3 likes per post on a 2,188-follower base.
- The single best-performing post in the public sample was the FTP webinar that named Kenneth Levey and Bryan Ridgway (10 likes, 3-5x the median).
- Zero member spotlights and zero original thought leadership in the visible window.
- Pattern: **named humans + specific technical topic** beats generic event-brand promos.

**Cadence shift:** Plan calls for 3 posts in week 2. Pair these with a 40-50% reduction in overall posting volume going forward, prioritizing the content types below.

---

## Post 1 — Thought Leadership

**Target:** Senior finance professionals (CFOs, controllers, VPs) at community banks and credit unions
**Format:** Text only (performs better organically than link posts)
**Best post day/time:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 9-11am CT

**Draft:**

Community bank finance teams are managing a challenge their predecessors didn't face at this scale: a rate environment that changes the math on nearly every product in the portfolio while margin pressure compounds from both sides.

The finance managers navigating this well aren't working alone. Peer benchmarking, shared frameworks, continuing education from people doing the same job at similar institutions. That isn't soft value. It's how faster, better-informed decisions get made.

That's what FMS is built for.

**Hashtags:** #CommunityBanking #FinancialManagement #CreditUnions #ProfessionalDevelopment

## Post 4 (NEW) — "Member in the Field" Template

**Format:** Photo of member + 5-7 line caption. Native LinkedIn post (no link).
**Why this is new:** Audit found zero member spotlights in the visible window. A 1,500+ member association sitting on this content is leaving the highest-trust, lowest-cost format completely unused. Stand up as a weekly series.

**Template:**

When [Member Name], [Title] at [Institution], [verb describing concrete challenge: walked into / inherited / was asked to lead] [specific situation], [he/she] turned to [specific FMS resource: a Q2 Call Report seminar / the ALCO peer group / the 2025 Forum].

What changed: [one specific outcome in one sentence].

"[One-sentence direct quote from member about what FMS gave them]."

That's the membership. People doing this work, using what we build, talking about what worked.

[Tag member's LinkedIn profile if they're open to it]

## Review Checklist

- [ ] No exclamation points
- [ ] No "we're thrilled to announce" or "excited to share" opener language
- [ ] No "amazing," "game-changing," or any superlative
- [ ] Post 1 does not mention FMS until the final line
- [ ] All posts are under 250 words (LinkedIn rewards concision)
- [ ] Post 4 uses a real member with their consent before posting

## Cadence Recommendation (Ongoing)

Per the audit, drop from ~12 posts/week to **6-8 posts/week** with this weekly mix:
- 2 carousel / document posts
- 1 native video
- 2 educational text posts authored by named FMS staff or board members
- 1 "Member in the Field" spotlight
- 1-2 event promos (named-human format only)

## LinkedIn Setup

Schedule Posts 1-4 in week 2 via LinkedIn's native scheduling tool. Tuesday / Wednesday / Friday morning slots perform best for B2B audiences in the FMS demographic.
