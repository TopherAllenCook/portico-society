# LinkedIn Admin Audit — Financial Managers Society

**Audit date:** 2026-05-25
**Method:** Direct LinkedIn Page Admin login via Playwright
**Period covered:** 90 days (Feb 23, 2026 – May 23, 2026) for content; 30 days for followers + competitors
**Audience:** Jennifer Lindberg + Alana Vartanian
**Status:** Complete on admin-only metrics. Companion to [audit-linkedin-public.md](audit-linkedin-public.md) and the deeper public re-audit at [audit-public-deep.md](audit-public-deep.md).

---

## TL;DR

1. **2,188 total followers** (confirms public audit).
2. **+29 new followers in last 30 days (+38.1% vs prior period).** Roughly 1 net new follower per day. That's a real growth signal, modest but positive.
3. **90-day reach: 22,858 impressions** across all posts. With 2,188 followers, that's ~10.4 impressions per follower over 90 days = 0.12 imp/follower/day. Below benchmark for engaged B2B pages.
4. **90-day engagement: 348 reactions + 10 comments + 14 reposts = 372 total engagements.** Engagement rate = **1.6% per impression** (below 2-3% B2B benchmark).
5. **Darling Consulting Group, an FMS Forum 2026 sponsor, has 15,514 followers — 7x FMS's count.** They posted 4 times in 30 days; FMS posted 46. **Darling gets ~10x the engagement per post with ~10% the posting volume.** This is the single sharpest competitive data point in the audit.
6. **Followers are concentrated in the Northeast and Chicago.** Top 5 metros (NYC, Boston, Chicago, Philly, Dallas-Fort Worth) account for ~33% of followers.

### Sample of recent posts (30-day window, partial — confirms the public audit pattern)

The Content engagement table shows ~12-15 posts visible in the 30-day default window, all "All followers" audience type, impressions per post ranging roughly 25 (low) to 144 (highest):
- "**With help from experts at Third Party Resources, Inc...**" (Document type — PDF carousel) — 124 impressions. **Confirms the public audit: document/carousel posts outperform single-image posts.**
- "**ALCO committees are being asked to make faster, higher-impact decisions...**" — 144 impressions.
- "**You're Invited: Join us in NOLA Next Month**" — 95 impressions (Forum 2026 promo).
- "FMS Forum Exhibitor & Sponsor" announcements (Fiserv, Piper Sandler, Crowe, Trintech) — typically 30-90 impressions each.
- "**FMS Members: Tap into Your Exclusive Members Only Community App**" — 25 impressions (lowest of visible posts; speaks to a small audience).

**Confirms public audit pattern:** Sponsor-announcement posts pull lower than thought-leadership or document carousels. The Document-type post outperformed all single-image posts in the visible window.

### Volume reality check

22,858 impressions over 90 days ÷ ~46 posts visible across the same window = **~497 impressions per post average**. For a 2,188-follower page, that means **each post is reaching roughly 23% of the follower base in raw impressions.** Most posts are reaching fewer than half their followers.

**Why:** LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes engagement. With ~1.6% engagement rate per impression, the algorithm pulls back distribution. Posting more often makes it worse, not better.

## 3. Competitor benchmark (the killer finding)

LinkedIn's "Compare growth" view (last 30 days) shows FMS against one direct comparator:

| Org | Followers | New (30d) | Posts (30d) | Comments | Comments / Post | Reactions |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Darling Consulting Group** | **15,514** | 81 | **4** | 4 | **1.0** | ~95 (est) |
| **Financial Managers Society** | **2,188** | 29 | **46** | 6 | **0.13** | 117 |

### What this tells the board

**Darling Consulting Group:**
- 7x the followers
- 11.5x fewer posts
- 8x the comments-per-post engagement
- Net followers added at ~2.8x FMS's pace (81 vs 29 new in 30 days)

**Per-post performance for Darling vs FMS (approx):**
- Comments: 1.0 / post vs 0.13 / post = **~8x**
- Reactions: ~24 / post vs ~2.5 / post = **~10x**
- New followers per post: 81/4 = 20 per post vs 29/46 = 0.6 per post = **~33x**

**The implication is unambiguous:** FMS's LinkedIn strategy is producing the inverse of what the algorithm rewards. Higher volume, lower engagement, slower follower growth. **Reducing posting cadence by 70-80% AND shifting to better content format would, by Darling's example, materially improve every metric on this page.**

Darling Consulting is also an FMS Forum 2026 sponsor and supplies named human experts (e.g., Darling consultants appeared as 2023 ALM Strategic Decisions webinar speakers). They are doing exactly what the public audit recommended: named-human content, lower volume, higher trust.

**Recommendation:** Match Darling's playbook. ~6-8 posts per week instead of ~12, all with a named human or member voice attached, prioritize document carousels and "Member in the Field" features (template already drafted in [docs/fms/linkedin/activation-posts.md](docs/fms/linkedin/activation-posts.md)).
